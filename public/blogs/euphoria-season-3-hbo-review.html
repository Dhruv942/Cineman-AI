Euphoria Season 3 Review: Ratings, What Changed & Is It Worth Watching?

Updated: April 24, 2026 9 min read

TL;DR

Euphoria Season 3 premiered April 12, 2026 on HBO/Max. Early episodes are tracking at 7.8–8.4 on IMDb and ~75% on Rotten Tomatoes. The season has course-corrected from Season 2's divisive sprawl with tighter plotting and more emotional focus. Worth watching if you were a fan — skip if Season 2 lost you.

Euphoria has always been a show that inspires strong reactions. Season 1 arrived in 2019 as one of the most visually distinctive and emotionally raw pieces of television in years. Season 2, released in 2022, divided audiences sharply: some found it a bold escalation, others a self-indulgent spiral. The four-year wait for Season 3 — extended by production complications, schedule shifts, and the 2023 strikes — gave the creative team time to reckon with that division. What has emerged in April 2026 is a Euphoria that feels simultaneously more assured and more restrained.

What Changed in Season 3

Creator Sam Levinson has spoken in pre-release interviews about approaching Season 3 differently. The production brought in additional writers to develop plotlines more collaboratively, addressing one of the main criticisms of Season 2 (that Levinson's singular vision sometimes produced narratively incoherent episodes). Early episodes show the results of this adjustment.

A More Focused Narrative

Season 2's eight episodes felt like they contained twelve episodes' worth of subplots, most of which were introduced and then abandoned. Season 3's first four episodes (which HBO made available to critics before the April 12 premiere) are notably more disciplined. Each episode has a clear emotional through-line. Character motivations are better established. Scenes that in Season 2 might have stretched to seven minutes of stylized montage are tighter and more purposeful.

This does not mean Euphoria has abandoned its visual maximalism. The cinematography (Marcell Rév returns as director of photography) is still extraordinary — the show remains among the most beautifully shot television being produced anywhere. But the imagery now feels more in service of story than in competition with it.

The Cast in Season 3

Zendaya returns as Rue, whose recovery arc continues to evolve. The character is older now — Season 3 moves the timeline forward slightly — and the show grapples with what Rue's addiction and its aftermath look like beyond the immediate crisis of Season 1 and 2. It is a more mature, less explosive version of the character, which will satisfy some viewers and frustrate others who found Zendaya's most raw, destructive moments the show's creative peak.

Sydney Sweeney (Cassie), Jacob Elordi (Nate), and Hunter Schafer (Jules) are all still central, though reporting during production suggested several cast members had renegotiated reduced episode counts. The ensemble feels slightly leaner than Season 2, which is mostly a positive development.

Tone and Subject Matter

Season 3 is still unambiguously adult content — explicit drug use, sexual content, and unflinching depictions of trauma are present. But the tone has shifted toward something slightly more contemplative. Where Season 2 sometimes felt like escalation for its own sake, Season 3's early episodes suggest a show more interested in the long-term psychological consequences of the events it depicts than in the visceral shock of depicting them.

Euphoria Season 3 Ratings

IMDb

Individual episode ratings on IMDb are tracking between 7.8 and 8.4 for the first four episodes, which is broadly in line with strong Season 1 episode-level ratings and above Season 2's more polarized individual scores (which ranged from 7.2 to 9.1). The aggregate season score will settle as the full run completes in May, but early trajectory is positive.

Rotten Tomatoes

Critics' consensus has opened at approximately 75% Fresh on RT, with the common thread in positive reviews being "a return to form" and in negative reviews being "still too slow to justify its runtime." The audience score is tracking several points higher than the critics' score, which typically indicates a fanbase that is more satisfied than critics acknowledge.

Critical Consensus

The clearest critical divide is between reviewers who find the Season 3 recalibration a step in the right direction and those who find it a retreat from the show's most ambitious instincts. Variety called the premiere "Euphoria finding its footing again." The Guardian found the first four episodes "impeccably made but emotionally cautious." Both assessments are consistent with the same viewing experience. Whether "more disciplined" reads as "improved" or "safer" depends on what you valued in Seasons 1 and 2.

Should You Watch Euphoria Season 3?

Watch if:

  • You liked Season 1 and felt Season 2 lost the plot — Season 3 addresses most of those complaints
  • You are a completionist who has been following these characters since 2019
  • You watch primarily for the visual craftsmanship and Zendaya's performance
  • You appreciate prestige drama that takes emotional and thematic risks

Skip if:

  • You have not seen Seasons 1 or 2 — Season 3 does not function as a standalone entry point
  • Explicit content, drug depictions, or sexual violence are dealbreakers for you
  • You gave up during Season 2 and have no particular attachment to the characters
  • You find the show's aesthetic maximalism self-indulgent regardless of story quality

Euphoria Season 3 in Context: HBO's 2026 Slate

Euphoria Season 3 is one of HBO's most significant launches of 2026. It arrives alongside the ongoing The Last of Us Season 2 (which concluded its run in early 2026) and precedes the much-anticipated House of the Dragon Season 3 in late 2026. HBO/Max is also the home of The White Lotus Season 3, which aired earlier this year to enormous critical acclaim.

Compared to that slate, Euphoria Season 3 is a mid-tier success so far — better received than the worst-case scenario, less transcendent than the best-case. It is firmly "good HBO drama" territory, which is a meaningful step up from Season 2's more troubled reception.

How to Watch Euphoria Season 3

Euphoria Season 3 is an HBO original and streams exclusively on Max (HBO Max). New episodes air Sundays at 9 PM ET, with same-night streaming availability. The season is 10 episodes running through late May 2026. A Max subscription is required — the show is not available on Netflix, Prime Video, or any other platform. All three seasons of Euphoria are available on Max for new viewers catching up.
