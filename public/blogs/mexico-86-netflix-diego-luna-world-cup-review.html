Mexico 86 Netflix: Diego Luna’s World Cup Comedy Review & Guide

Updated: May 29, 2026 9 min read

TL;DR

Mexico 86 drops on Netflix on June 5, 2026. Diego Luna stars in the sports comedy about the chaotic 1983–1986 scramble to organize the World Cup after Colombia pulled out. The 1986 World Cup almost didn't happen in Mexico. Colombia was the original host, awarded the tournament in 1974. By 1982, economic collapse forced Colombia to withdraw. Mexico had less than three years to prepare. The country had to upgrade stadiums in nine cities, build broadcast infrastructure for a global audience, navigate a 1985 earthquake that killed thousands and devastated Mexico City, and still deliver a tournament. They did it. It became the World Cup of Diego Maradona's “Hand of God,” his goal-of-the-century against England, and Argentina's championship.

Mexico 86 is the comedy about how that tournament got organized. Diego Luna plays the sports executive in the middle of the chaos. Netflix is dropping it on June 5, 2026 — timed perfectly to the buildup of the 2026 World Cup, which kicks off in North America just weeks later.

What the Film Is About

The story dramatizes a real, almost-impossible logistics challenge. In 1982, Colombia — mid-debt-crisis and politically destabilized — informed FIFA that it could not host the 1986 World Cup. FIFA scrambled. The United States made a bid. Canada made a bid. Brazil made a bid. Mexico's proposal was the strongest, even though Mexico had hosted the 1970 tournament just 16 years earlier, even though no country had ever hosted twice that close together, even though Mexico had less than three years to prepare what is normally a six-year build.

The film follows a fictional executive at the Mexican federation (Luna) tasked with making it happen. He has to manage FIFA delegates who don't believe Mexico can deliver, government officials who keep changing the budget, stadium architects who refuse to talk to each other, and a press corps that smells a disaster. Then, in September 1985, a magnitude-8.0 earthquake hits Mexico City and damages two of the tournament's planned venues. The final third of the film deals with that pivot.

It is, structurally, a hangout-comedy version of Argo — a real-world logistics-and-diplomacy nightmare played for laughs without losing its respect for what actually happened.

Why Netflix Is Releasing It Now

The 2026 FIFA World Cup begins on June 11, 2026, hosted jointly by Mexico, the United States, and Canada. It is the largest World Cup in history — 48 teams, 104 matches, 16 host cities. The opening ceremony is scheduled for Mexico City's iconic Estadio Azteca, the same stadium that hosted the 1986 final.

Netflix dropping Mexico 86 on June 5 puts it in front of audiences six days before the new tournament kicks off. The story is about Mexico hosting the World Cup. The country hosting the new World Cup is Mexico (and partners). The timing isn't subtle. It also isn't unwelcome — this is one of the rare cases where a streamer's release calendar matches a real cultural moment perfectly.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does Mexico 86 come out on Netflix?

June 5, 2026 — worldwide. The release is timed to the buildup of the 2026 FIFA World Cup hosted by Mexico, USA, and Canada.

Is Mexico 86 based on a true story?

Yes. It is loosely inspired by the real 1982–1986 events when Colombia withdrew as host and Mexico stepped in with less than three years to deliver the tournament.

Who else is in Mexico 86 besides Diego Luna?

Mexican stage and television actors fill out the supporting cast as FIFA delegates, journalists, and government officials. Luna anchors as the sports executive.

Is Mexico 86 in Spanish or English?

Primarily Spanish, with English dub and subtitles available. Watching in the original Spanish is recommended — the comic timing relies on the original performances.
