Star City: Apple TV+’s For All Mankind Spinoff Explained

Updated: May 9, 2026 10 min read

TL;DR

Star City is Apple TV+’s long-awaited spinoff of For All Mankind, set in the same alternate-history universe but told from the Soviet side of the never-ending space race. Same creative DNA (Ronald D. Moore, Ben Nedivi, Matt Wolpert), different perspective. It is one of Apple TV+’s biggest 2026 launches. Watch ratings on every Apple TV+ title with CineMan AI.

If you have not watched For All Mankind, you have been missing one of the most ambitious sci-fi shows of the last decade. The premise: what if the Soviets had landed on the moon first, and the space race never ended? Across four seasons, the show tracked how that one divergence reshaped technology, politics, and human progress through the late 20th century into the 21st. It is also a notoriously slept-on show — a victim of being on Apple TV+, where prestige series often live in cultural quiet.

Now Apple is doing the smartest possible thing: doubling down. Star City is the spinoff that flips the camera. Same universe, same alternate history, same propulsive engineering-thriller energy — but seen from inside the Soviet space program. Here is everything we know.

What Is Star City?

Star City — in Russian, Zvyozdny Gorodok — is a real, still-functioning town outside Moscow that has trained Soviet and Russian cosmonauts since the early 1960s. It is one of the most secretive and storied places in space history. The Apple TV+ series takes its name from this real location and uses it as the geographic and emotional anchor of the show.

Where For All Mankind centers on NASA, Houston, and the American astronaut corps, Star City takes place inside the Soviet cosmonaut program during the same alternate-history span. We see the cosmonauts who beat the Americans to the moon. We see the engineers under crushing political pressure. We see the families who live inside a closed military town built around space.

Why This Spinoff Matters

The decision to set the show on the Soviet side is the entire point. For All Mankind has always shown the Russians as worthy adversaries — competent, smart, ambitious — but ultimately as antagonists viewed from outside. Star City is going to invert that. It is asking American audiences to spend hours inside the lives, marriages, ambitions, and grief of people the parent show could only gesture at.

For an alternate-history series whose central thesis is that competition makes humanity better, this is the natural next move. You cannot argue that competition is generative if you only show one side of the competition.

The Creative Team

Star City is co-created by Ben Nedivi and Matt Wolpert, the writing-producing partnership that ran the writers’ room of the parent series for most of its run. Ronald D. Moore — co-creator of For All Mankind, showrunner of the modern Battlestar Galactica, and a veteran of Outlander — is an executive producer.

This continuity matters. Spinoffs that drift from their parent show’s creative DNA are usually the spinoffs that fail. Star City inherits the same engineers-and-grease-stains realism that For All Mankind earned its reputation on. Apple has not handed the universe to a generic showrunner.

What to Expect from the Show

Engineering-thriller realism.

For All Mankind is famously rigorous about technical detail. Star City will be the same. Expect long, tense scenes of mission control, burn calculations, and hardware that fails for reasons you can mostly understand if you pay attention.

Cold War paranoia, but humanized.

The Soviet space program operated under levels of political pressure American audiences will find shocking. Engineers were arrested for failures. Defectors were hunted. Any successful mission could be repurposed as state propaganda overnight. Star City is going to live inside that environment.

New characters, occasional crossover.

Most of the cast is new, focusing on a generational ensemble in the cosmonaut corps and the engineering and political layers around them. Crossover with For All Mankind characters will be limited but present. Expect at least one familiar face per season.

Multi-season ambition.

This is not a limited series. Apple has positioned Star City as a flagship, multi-season project meant to track Soviet space history through the same decades the parent show covers, with eventual divergence as the alternate-history clock keeps running.

How to Prep If You Have Not Watched For All Mankind

You can absolutely jump into Star City cold, but if you want to prepare, here is the lean version:

  • Season 1 of For All Mankind — The diverging point. Soviets land first, NASA scrambles to catch up.
  • Skim seasons 2–3 — The 1980s and 1990s in the alternate timeline. Mars, lunar bases, geopolitics.
  • Watch the season 4 premiere — The 2000s alternate present, where Star City’s back half will likely overlap.

You can also just watch all four seasons. They are excellent.

Where Star City Fits in Apple TV+’s 2026

Apple TV+’s 2026 has three major bets: Severance Season 3, Foundation Season 3, and Star City. The first two are returning hits. Star City is the new entry. If the show lands, Apple finally has the prestige sci-fi trifecta it has been building toward since the platform launched. If it does not, the For All Mankind universe loses a lot of its forward momentum.

Bottom Line

Star City is the spinoff For All Mankind needed and probably the show that will finally get this universe the audience it deserves. Same creative team, harder-edged setting, fewer narrative crutches. If you are an Apple TV+ subscriber, mark the calendar.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Star City about?

Star City is the Soviet-side spinoff of For All Mankind. It follows the cosmonaut program in the same alternate-history universe where the USSR landed on the moon first and the space race never ended.

Do I need to watch For All Mankind first?

Helpful but not required. The show is built so new viewers can jump in cold. For All Mankind fans will get extra context and occasional character crossovers.

Is Ronald D. Moore showrunning Star City?

Moore is an executive producer. The day-to-day showrunner duties are with Ben Nedivi and Matt Wolpert, who ran the writers’ room on much of the parent show.

When does Star City premiere?

Apple TV+ has confirmed a 2026 premiere as part of its tentpole slate, with episodes scheduled to roll out across the latter half of the year.
