New on Netflix in May 2026: Every Movie and Show Worth Watching

Updated: April 24, 2026 10 min read

TL;DR

May 2026 on Netflix is headlined by The Boroughs (supernatural thriller from the Stranger Things creators), A Good Girl's Guide to Murder Season 2 (Emma Myers returns as Pip), Lord of the Flies (Netflix's original adaptation), Remarkably Bright Creatures (Sally Field, Lewis Pullman), and The Four Seasons (Tina Fey comedy). May 2026 is one of Netflix's strongest months of the year so far. The platform is launching several high-profile originals across drama, thriller, animation, and comedy — plus a live roast event and a major MMA documentary. Here is a complete breakdown of everything arriving on Netflix in May, ranked and annotated for your browsing convenience.

The Biggest New Netflix Originals in May 2026

The Boroughs

Genre: Supernatural Thriller  |  Why watch: Created by the team behind Stranger Things, The Boroughs is Netflix's most anticipated series launch of May. The show follows a tight-knit community in a seemingly ordinary American suburb where residents begin experiencing coordinated supernatural events. Early descriptions position it as a cross between Stranger Things' nostalgia-drenched dread and the slow-burn tension of The Haunting of Hill House. If the Stranger Things DNA translates, this could be the show that defines early summer 2026 viewing.

The fact that Netflix is giving The Boroughs its flagship promotional push for May — full billboard campaigns, premiere screenings, multiple trailer drops — signals significant internal confidence. Watch this one early before the discourse gets spoiler-heavy.

A Good Girl's Guide to Murder: Season 2

Genre: Teen Mystery / Crime Drama  |  Why watch: Season 1 was a quiet hit for Netflix, turning Holly Jackson's YA mystery novels into an addictive binge. Emma Myers returns as Pip Fitz-Amobi, now dealing with the psychological fallout of the first season's investigation while pursuing a new missing-person case. The show finds a strong balance between teen drama and genuine procedural tension. Season 2 adapts the second book in Jackson's trilogy, which fans consider the darkest and most emotionally complex of the three. Full season review up once it drops.

Lord of the Flies

Genre: Drama / Survival Thriller  |  Why watch: William Golding's novel has been adapted twice for film (1963, 1990) with mixed results. Netflix's 2026 version gets a full series treatment, which gives the story space to develop each boy's psychological unraveling with more depth than a two-hour film allows. The premise — a group of stranded schoolboys forming violent tribal hierarchies in the absence of adult authority — is evergreen in its horror. How Netflix updates the story for a contemporary setting will be the central question going into release.

Devil May Cry: Season 2

Genre: Animated / Action  |  Why watch: Netflix's animated adaptation of Capcom's Devil May Cry was a polarizing first season — visually impressive and stylistically bold, but divisive on story. Dante returns for Season 2 with early promotional material suggesting a broader scope and higher-stakes demon conflict. If you are a fan of the games or enjoyed Season 1's aesthetic, this is an easy addition to your queue. If you bounced off Season 1's pacing, Season 2 will likely not convert you.

The Four Seasons

Genre: Comedy Drama  |  Why watch: Created by Tina Fey, Lang Fisher, and Tracey Wigfield — the team behind Brooklyn Nine-Nine and 30 Rock adjacents — The Four Seasons is a comedy series about three couples navigating friendships, relationships, and middle age across four seasonal trips together. The pedigree is strong. Tina Fey's track record for biting, dialogue-driven comedy is unmatched on television. This is a candidate for the best adult comedy Netflix launches in the first half of 2026.

Nemesis: Season 1

Genre: Crime Drama / Thriller  |  Why watch: From the showrunner of Power comes a new thriller that trades Power's New York drug empire setting for something reportedly more political and conspiratorial. Details are limited pre-release, but the Power pedigree carries weight with that show's massive fanbase. This is one to watch if you liked Power, Ghost, or any of the Power Universe spinoffs.

New Movies on Netflix in May 2026

Remarkably Bright Creatures

Genre: Drama  |  Stars: Sally Field, Lewis Pullman  |  Why watch: Based on Shelby Van Pelt's bestselling novel about an elderly woman working at an aquarium who forms an unlikely friendship with a giant Pacific octopus, Remarkably Bright Creatures is one of the most genuinely unusual premises of 2026. Sally Field's return to a major dramatic role after several years of limited screen time is the headline casting. Lewis Pullman (Top Gun: Maverick, Strangers) provides the emotional counterweight as a young man searching for answers about his past. This is the kind of character-driven adult drama that Netflix has historically underinvested in. Strong advance word suggests it delivers.

Swapped

Genre: Animated Feature  |  Voices: Michael B. Jordan, Juno Temple  |  Why watch: Netflix's animated slate has been inconsistent, but Swapped — a body-swapping comedy featuring two animals who must navigate each other's worlds — has strong voice cast energy and a premise with family-friendly broad appeal. Michael B. Jordan doing animated comedy is a left-turn from his usual prestige drama work, which makes this interesting if nothing else. One for the household queue.

Live Events on Netflix in May 2026

The Roast of Kevin Hart (Live)

Netflix Live continues its celebrity roast format with Kevin Hart as the subject, hosted by Shane Gillis from The Kia Forum. Hart's roastability is exceptionally high given his career arc, public controversies, and near-universal likability — a combination that produces strong roast television. The live format adds genuine stakes: anything can happen, and the possibility of an unscripted moment going viral is part of the appeal.

Countdown: Rousey vs. Carano

Netflix's MMA documentary series returns to cover training camp ahead of the May 16 fight between Ronda Rousey and Gina Carano — one of the most commercially loaded bouts in women's MMA history. Whether you follow MMA or not, the documentary format Netflix uses for fight camps has consistently produced compelling character portraits.
