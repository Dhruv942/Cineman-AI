The Cast

Milly Alcock as Kara Zor-El / Supergirl

Alcock's breakout was Young Rhaenyra in House of the Dragon Season 1. Her brief Kara cameo in Superman (2025) — the closing-act sequence where she retrieves Krypto — introduced a meaningfully different Supergirl than previous adaptations: drunker, more cynical, more lethal. Woman of Tomorrow is built around that interpretation.

Jason Momoa as Lobo

The casting that broke the internet. Momoa publicly campaigned for Lobo for nearly a decade before James Gunn made it happen. Lobo — the Czarnian bounty hunter, the universe's loudest violent biker — is a tonal departure from Momoa's Aquaman in every way that matters. The bet is that he's spent his entire career secretly auditioning for this role.

What the Story Is About

The film adapts Tom King and Bilquis Evely's eight-issue comic Supergirl: Woman of Tomorrow (DC, 2021–2022). The premise: Kara is celebrating her 21st birthday on a planet under a red sun, deliberately powerless. A young alien girl named Ruthye arrives and begs Kara to help her hunt down the man who killed her father. What starts as a side quest becomes a multi-planet revenge journey that forces Kara to confront the survivor's-guilt grief that being Superman's invulnerable cousin has let her bury for years.

It is a road movie. It is a Western. It is the saddest Supergirl story ever published, and it is the one James Gunn picked — signaling that the DCU is going to lean into character work over universe-building, at least in this opening run.

Where Supergirl Fits in the DCU

James Gunn's DCU is structured as three chapters. We are in Chapter One: Gods and Monsters. The release order so far:

  1. Creature Commandos (animated series, HBO Max, 2024)
  2. Superman (theatrical, 2025) — David Corenswet's debut
  3. Peacemaker Season 2 (HBO Max, 2025)
  4. Supergirl: Woman of Tomorrow (theatrical, June 2026)
  5. Clayface (theatrical, September 2026)
  6. The Authority (theatrical, 2027 — in production)

Supergirl is the second theatrical release and the first to introduce a major non-Earth setting at scale. Its job is twofold: prove the box-office can repeat without Superman's name on the marquee, and plant the cosmic story-engine that the Authority and later films will need.
