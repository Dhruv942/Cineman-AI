Best Anime Movies & Series to Watch in 2026 [Streaming With Ratings]

TL;DR

Our 18 picks cover the best anime movies (Spirited Away, Your Name, Akira, Perfect Blue, and more) and series (Attack on Titan, Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood, Death Note, Vinland Saga, and more) with IMDb and RT ratings and where to stream each one. Anime is no longer a niche interest. It is one of the fastest-growing content categories on every major streaming platform, and some of the highest-rated content on IMDb and Rotten Tomatoes comes from Japanese animation. But navigating the anime landscape can be intimidating. The volume of content is enormous, the genre classifications are unfamiliar to newcomers, and quality varies wildly from masterpiece to forgettable.

This guide covers the ten best anime movies and eight best anime series currently available on streaming platforms, with IMDb and Rotten Tomatoes ratings for every pick. Whether you are a lifelong anime fan looking for your next watch or someone who has never watched anime and wants to know where to start, this list has you covered.

Spirited Away (IMDb 8.6, RT 97%) is the undisputed masterpiece of anime cinema. Hayao Miyazaki's story of a young girl trapped in a spirit world bathhouse is a visual wonder that works on multiple levels — as a children's adventure, as a coming-of-age allegory, and as a meditation on greed and environmentalism. It won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature and remains the highest-grossing film in Japanese box office history. If you watch one anime film in your life, make it this one.

Your Name (IMDb 8.4, RT 98%) is Makoto Shinkai's romantic fantasy about two teenagers who mysteriously begin swapping bodies across time and space. The premise sounds lighthearted, but the film builds toward an emotional climax that is devastating in the best possible way. The animation quality is breathtaking — Shinkai renders Tokyo and rural Japan with a luminous beauty that makes every frame feel like a painting.

Akira (IMDb 8.0, RT 90%) is the film that introduced anime to Western audiences and it has lost none of its power. Set in a dystopian Neo-Tokyo in 2019, a motorcycle gang member develops telekinetic powers that threaten to destroy the city. The animation, hand-drawn in 1988, remains more visually impressive than most modern anime. The cyberpunk aesthetic, the body horror, and the sheer ambition of the storytelling make it an essential film regardless of genre preferences.

Princess Mononoke (IMDb 8.4, RT 93%) is Miyazaki's environmental epic about the conflict between industrial civilization and the natural world. A young prince caught between a mining town and the spirits of the forest must find a way to make peace before both sides destroy each other. It is Miyazaki's most morally complex film — there are no true villains, only competing needs and perspectives.

Perfect Blue (IMDb 8.0, RT 80%) is Satoshi Kon's psychological thriller about a pop idol who retires from music to become an actress and begins losing her grip on reality as a stalker closes in. The film blurs the line between reality and delusion so effectively that you genuinely cannot tell what is real by the third act. It was a direct influence on Darren Aronofsky's Black Swan and Requiem for a Dream.

Ghost in the Shell (IMDb 8.0, RT 96%) asks what it means to be human in a world where consciousness can be digitized and bodies are interchangeable. Major Motoko Kusanagi, a cybernetic government agent, hunts a mysterious hacker while questioning the nature of her own identity. The film's philosophical depth and visual innovation set the template for cyberpunk storytelling and directly influenced The Matrix.

Paprika (IMDb 7.7, RT 86%) is Satoshi Kon's exploration of dream technology gone wrong. A device that allows therapists to enter patients' dreams is stolen, and dreams begin leaking into reality. The visual imagination on display is staggering — Kon creates dream sequences that are surreal, terrifying, and beautiful in rapid succession. Christopher Nolan has acknowledged Paprika's influence on Inception.

Grave of the Fireflies (IMDb 8.5, RT 100%) is the most emotionally devastating animated film ever made. Two children struggle to survive in the final months of World War II after their mother is killed in an air raid. The film does not manipulate — it simply shows, with unflinching honesty, what war does to the most vulnerable. The 100% Rotten Tomatoes score reflects universal recognition of its power. Be warned: this film will break you.

A Silent Voice (IMDb 8.1, RT 95%) is a redemption story about a young man who bullied a deaf girl in elementary school and, years later, seeks to make amends. It is a nuanced exploration of bullying, disability, self-hatred, and the possibility of forgiveness. The animation by Kyoto Animation is gorgeous, and the emotional journey is earned through patient, honest storytelling.

Suzume (IMDb 7.6, RT 96%) is Makoto Shinkai's latest film about a teenager who discovers doors that, if left open, release catastrophic earthquakes. She embarks on a journey across Japan to close them. Like Your Name, Suzume blends fantasy adventure with genuine emotional weight, and Shinkai's visual artistry continues to set the standard for anime cinema.

Attack on Titan (IMDb 9.1) is the defining anime series of the past decade. What begins as a straightforward survival story about humanity fighting giant humanoid creatures evolves into one of the most complex geopolitical narratives in any medium. The story's willingness to challenge its own heroes, reverse moral alignments, and confront the cycle of violence with genuine intellectual seriousness sets it apart from anything else in anime. The final season concludes the story with a scope that rivals Game of Thrones at its best.

Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood (IMDb 9.1) follows two brothers who use forbidden alchemy to try to resurrect their dead mother, with catastrophic consequences, and embark on a quest to restore their bodies. Brotherhood is often cited as the most perfectly structured long-form anime — every subplot connects, every character arc resolves, and the finale is genuinely satisfying. It is a masterclass in serialized storytelling.

Death Note (IMDb 9.0) is the ultimate cat-and-mouse thriller. A high school student discovers a notebook that kills anyone whose name is written in it and decides to use it to rid the world of criminals. The resulting battle of wits between the student and a mysterious detective is tense, twisty, and addictive. The first 25 episodes are essentially perfect. At only 37 episodes, it is one of the most time-efficient anime series on this list.

Steins;Gate (IMDb 8.8) is one of the best time-travel stories in any medium. A group of eccentric friends accidentally invent a way to send messages to the past and gradually realize the devastating consequences of changing the timeline. The first half is a slow build of character development and mystery. The second half is an emotional rollercoaster that pays off every thread the first half established.

Vinland Saga (IMDb 8.8) follows a young Viking warrior consumed by revenge who gradually discovers that violence solves nothing. The first season is a brutal Viking action epic. The second season pivots into a meditation on pacifism, slavery, and what it means to build rather than destroy. It is one of the most thematically ambitious anime series ever made, and the tonal shift between seasons is breathtaking.

Mob Psycho 100 (IMDb 8.6) is created by the same author as One Punch Man but is a far better show. A middle school psychic with immense powers is taught by his con-artist mentor that psychic abilities do not make him special — kindness, effort, and genuine human connection do. It is hilarious, visually creative, and surprisingly emotional. The animation pushes the medium's boundaries in every episode.

Cowboy Bebop (IMDb 8.9) is the coolest anime ever made. A crew of bounty hunters drift through the solar system in their spaceship, chasing bounties and running from their pasts. The jazz-infused soundtrack, the noir-influenced visual style, and the episodic structure create something that feels completely unique. Each crew member's backstory is revealed gradually, building toward a finale that is both inevitable and devastating.

Jujutsu Kaisen (IMDb 8.6) is the current standard-bearer for action anime. A high school student swallows the finger of a powerful demon and is enrolled in a school for sorcerers who fight cursed spirits. The action choreography is among the best in anime history, the power system is creative and well-defined, and the show maintains a sense of genuine stakes that many action anime lose.

The Newcomer Gateway Sequence

If you have never watched anime and want a progression that eases you in, here is the recommended order:

  1. Spirited Away (Movie) — The most universally accessible anime film. If you enjoy animated films at all, you will love this.
  2. Death Note (Series, 37 eps) — A thriller that does not rely on anime tropes. It hooks non-anime fans immediately with its premise and pacing.
  3. Your Name (Movie) — Introduces you to modern anime's visual peak and emotional storytelling.
  4. Attack on Titan (Series, 94 eps) — The gateway into long-form anime. The first episode's hook is one of the most effective in television history.
  5. Cowboy Bebop (Series, 26 eps) — Shows you how stylistically diverse anime can be. Feels more like a live-action show than traditional anime.
  6. Perfect Blue (Movie) — Demonstrates that anime can be sophisticated adult cinema, not just action and fantasy.

By the end of this sequence, you will have a sense of anime's range and can follow your preferences into specific genres: more Ghibli for fantasy, more thriller for Death Note-style tension, more action for shonen, or more experimental for Satoshi Kon's filmography.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best anime movie for beginners?

Spirited Away (IMDb 8.6, RT 97%) is the best starting point for anime newcomers. It is universally accessible, visually stunning, and tells a story that resonates with all ages. If you prefer something more modern, Your Name (IMDb 8.4, RT 98%) is equally accessible.

Where can I watch anime for free or on streaming?

Netflix has a growing anime library including exclusive titles. Crunchyroll is the largest dedicated anime platform with some free content. Max (HBO) streams Studio Ghibli films in the US. Prime Video carries select titles. CineMan AI shows streaming availability on anime titles across platforms.

What is the highest-rated anime of all time?

On IMDb, Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood holds the top anime series rating at 9.1. For movies, Spirited Away (8.6 IMDb, 97% RT) is the most acclaimed, having won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature in 2003.

Is anime just for kids?

Absolutely not. Films like Perfect Blue and Ghost in the Shell are sophisticated adult thrillers. Series like Attack on Titan and Vinland Saga contain graphic violence and complex moral themes. Anime is a medium, not a genre — it spans the full spectrum from children's entertainment to mature adult content.
