Stranger Things has been the defining streaming-era show. Five seasons, billions of hours watched, and a cultural footprint that turned Eggo waffles, Kate Bush, and Dungeons & Dragons into quarterly trending searches. With the show now wrapped (and the final season in 2025 pulling record numbers), the gap it leaves behind is enormous.

The good news: the streaming era has produced more "shows like Stranger Things" than any other format. Below are 15 of the strongest, ranked by composite IMDb and Rotten Tomatoes scores.

Pick by What You Loved About Stranger Things

If you loved the kid-and-monster ensemble

The OA, Locke & Key, Wednesday, and The Boroughs all build their stories around a small group navigating something inexplicable. The OA is the closest emotional cousin — if you loved the way Stranger Things made friendship the actual stakes, The OA is your show. Locke & Key trades the kids for slightly older teens but keeps the supernatural-as-coming-of-age framework intact.

If you loved the supernatural mystery

Dark, From, Outer Range, and Severance are the picks for viewers who came for the mystery box. Dark is the masterpiece — a German time-travel show that is denser, smarter, and arguably better than Stranger Things itself. Severance applies the same "something is wrong here" logic to a workplace sci-fi setting, with Apple TV+ production values that are genuinely cinematic.

If you loved the small-town setting

Hawkins, Indiana is a character in Stranger Things. Sharp Objects (Wind Gap, Missouri), Castle Rock (Castle Rock, Maine), From (the unnamed town with the monsters), and Outer Range (Wabang, Wyoming) all make their settings load-bearing in the same way. Sharp Objects is the most prestige of the bunch — eight episodes, Amy Adams, almost no monsters, all dread.

If you loved the '80s nostalgia

This is the hardest box to fill. Most "shows like Stranger Things" inherit the ensemble or the supernatural without the period setting. Cobra Kai is the closest direct '80s-callback show. The Goldbergs lives in the same era. Yellowjackets has a 1996 timeline that hits similar nostalgia notes. None capture the specific Spielberg-Carpenter-King energy that defines Stranger Things, because that aesthetic is genuinely hard to do without slipping into pastiche.

If you loved the horror element

Lovecraft Country, From, The Haunting of Hill House, Castle Rock, and Yellowjackets push further into horror than Stranger Things does. Hill House is the gold standard if you want Mike Flanagan's brand of family-trauma-as-haunting. Yellowjackets is the most psychologically vicious. Lovecraft Country uses period horror to make sharp arguments about race in 1950s America.

Why Stranger Things Was Hard to Replicate

Three things are genuinely hard to manufacture:

First, the cast. The Duffer Brothers got lucky with the original kids (and developed them across five seasons in a way that built genuine loyalty). Most "shows like Stranger Things" cast a similar ensemble and discover that the chemistry just is not there.

Second, the tone calibration. Stranger Things is scary enough to feel like horror, light enough to feel like a family show, and warm enough to feel like a hangout. That balance is more delicate than it looks. Most imitators tip toward one register and lose the others.

Third, the Netflix marketing engine. Stranger Things became a cultural phenomenon partly because Netflix pushed it harder than any other show in its history. Whether The Boroughs (Netflix's chosen heir) gets the same treatment is the most-watched question in Netflix programming right now.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best show like Stranger Things?

The OA, Dark, and Wednesday are the three strongest matches across the supernatural mystery, ensemble cast, and binge-friendly format that defines Stranger Things.

Where can I watch shows like Stranger Things?

Most are on Netflix (The OA, Dark, Wednesday, Locke & Key, The Boroughs). HBO Max has Lovecraft Country and Sharp Objects. Hulu has Castle Rock. Prime Video has Outer Range.

Is The Boroughs the next Stranger Things?

Netflix has positioned The Boroughs as a spiritual successor — supernatural mystery, ensemble cast, town-as-character setting. Critical reception has been positive.

Are these shows scary like Stranger Things?

Some are gentler (Wednesday, Locke & Key), some are similar in intensity, and some go further (Lovecraft Country, Sharp Objects, From). Check ratings before showing to younger viewers.
