Spider-Man: Brand New Day (2026): Everything We Know

Published: May 15, 2026 11 min read

TL;DR

Spider-Man: Brand New Day releases July 31, 2026. Tom Holland returns as Peter Parker, picking up after No Way Home's memory-wipe ending. Jon Bernthal's Punisher and a redesigned Scorpion lead the villain lineup. Smaller in scale, grounded in street-level New York, and reportedly the most personal Spidey film yet. It has been five years since Tom Holland's Peter Parker stood on a New York rooftop, anonymous and forgotten by everyone he loved, at the end of Spider-Man: No Way Home. That cliffhanger ending was always going to be a hard one to follow. Spider-Man: Brand New Day, releasing in theaters on July 31, 2026, is the answer.

Here is everything confirmed, leaked, and reasonably speculated about the next Spider-Man film.

Release Date and Format

  • Theatrical release: July 31, 2026 (worldwide, including IMAX)
  • Streaming release: Disney+ approximately 90 days after theatrical (likely late October 2026)
  • Runtime: Reportedly around 2 hours 15 minutes
  • Rating: Expected PG-13, though Marvel has pushed for a darker tone

Plot: What We Know

The official synopsis is intentionally vague: "Peter Parker fights to find his footing in a New York that no longer remembers him." That premise is borrowed directly from the 2007 Brand New Day comics arc, in which a magical reset stripped Peter's marriage and identity from the Marvel Universe canon — a deeply controversial story at the time, and one the MCU now seems to be rehabilitating with more care.

What this likely means in practice:

  • Peter is alone. No Aunt May. No MJ remembering him. No Ned remembering him. No Stark Industries safety net.
  • He is broke. He is still in his early twenties. He is figuring out how to be Spider-Man without any institutional support.
  • The film leans street-level — closer in tone to Daredevil: Born Again than to the Avengers films.
  • It is positioned as the start of a new Spider-Man trilogy, not a continuation of the old one.

Cast

Confirmed and heavily rumored:

  • Tom Holland as Peter Parker / Spider-Man
  • Zendaya as MJ — reportedly in a supporting role, with a complicated reintroduction since she does not remember Peter
  • Jacob Batalon as Ned — same situation
  • Jon Bernthal as Frank Castle / The Punisher — carrying over from Daredevil: Born Again
  • Sadie Sink in an undisclosed but heavily-speculated-about role (the leading guess: Jean Grey-style mutant power awakening, or alternatively Mary Jane Watson)
  • Mark Ruffalo as Bruce Banner / Hulk — mentor figure, per multiple trade reports
  • Stephen Graham as a redesigned Scorpion
  • Tramell Tillman in an undisclosed role

Director and Creative Team

  • Director: Destin Daniel Cretton (Shang-Chi). Known for grounded action and family drama — a deliberate pick over a more spectacle-driven director.
  • Writers: Chris McKenna and Erik Sommers, who wrote all three previous Holland-era Spider-Man films.
  • Producers: Kevin Feige and Amy Pascal — the standard MCU/Sony partnership.

Cretton's hire signaled the tone shift from the start. Shang-Chi was the most emotionally grounded MCU film of Phase Four. Brand New Day is reportedly leaning into that same register: less multiverse, more loneliness.

How It Connects to the MCU

Brand New Day arrives between Avengers: Doomsday (releasing earlier in 2026) and Avengers: Secret Wars. Its job in the larger MCU machine is to re-establish Spider-Man as a character before he is asked to share the screen with the entire Avengers roster again.

Expected connections:

  • Daredevil: Born Again — the Punisher crossover formally bridges the Spider-Man franchise with the street-level Marvel TV slate.
  • Hulk solo prospects — Ruffalo's expanded role here may be the bridge to the long-rumored World War Hulk film.
  • Avengers: Secret Wars — the ending is reportedly designed to leave Peter in a specific emotional place that sets up his Avengers role.

How It Compares to the Past Spider-Man Films

The MCU now has eight live-action Spider-Man films across three actors (Maguire, Garfield, Holland) and two universes (Sony's Sinister Six adjacent slate and the MCU's Holland-led trilogy). On IMDb, here is roughly where the Holland-era films sit:

  • Spider-Man: No Way Home (2021) — 8.2 IMDb / 93% RT
  • Spider-Man: Far From Home (2019) — 7.4 IMDb / 90% RT
  • Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017) — 7.4 IMDb / 92% RT

Should You Be Excited?

The pieces are right. A grounded director, a smaller-scale story, a returning lead actor at the peak of his star power, a villain (Punisher) who has the audience on his side, and a built-in narrative engine in the memory-wipe premise.

The risk is the MCU itself. Phase Five and Phase Six have been uneven, and audience trust in Marvel as a guarantor of quality is at its lowest point since 2008. Brand New Day's success or failure may say less about Spider-Man and more about whether the broader MCU project still works.

One number to watch: the opening weekend box office. Anything above $200M domestic confirms Spider-Man's draw is undamaged. Anything below $150M and Marvel has bigger problems than this film.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does Spider-Man: Brand New Day release?

It hits theaters worldwide on July 31, 2026, with a Disney+ release expected approximately 90 days later.

Is Tom Holland returning as Spider-Man?

Yes. Brand New Day is his fourth solo MCU Spider-Man film.

Do I need to watch No Way Home first?

Strongly recommended. Brand New Day is a direct continuation of No Way Home's memory-erasure ending.

Who is the villain in Brand New Day?

The Punisher (Jon Bernthal) and a redesigned Scorpion (Stephen Graham) are confirmed antagonists. Bruce Banner / Hulk plays a major supporting role.
