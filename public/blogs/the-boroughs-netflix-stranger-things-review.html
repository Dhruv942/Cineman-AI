The Boroughs Netflix Review (2026): Stranger Things Team's New Show

Published: May 1, 2026 10 min read

TL;DR

The Boroughs premieres on Netflix May 21, 2026 — an eight-episode supernatural thriller from the Stranger Things production team set in a deceptively peaceful Pennsylvania retirement community where the residents start vanishing. If you have been waiting for the next Stranger Things-shaped hole in your watchlist, May 21 is circled on the calendar. The Boroughs is Netflix's new supernatural thriller from the production team behind their flagship genre franchise, and on paper it has all the ingredients of a slow-burn hit: an isolated, picturesque setting, an ensemble cast, a creeping mystery that pulls in unlikely heroes, and the unmistakable aesthetic fingerprint of producers who understand exactly how to build a world worth bingeing.

What Is The Boroughs About?

Set in a manicured Pennsylvania retirement community called Wyndridge, The Boroughs introduces us to a group of seniors whose orderly lives are upended when residents begin disappearing under impossible circumstances. The official Netflix synopsis hints at "an ancient force" stirring beneath the floral-bordered walking paths, and early trailers lean into the contrast between Wyndridge's almost suspiciously cheerful surface and something genuinely wrong simmering underneath.

What makes the show feel different from other recent supernatural thrillers is the cast. Instead of teenagers riding bikes, we get a group of retirees with full lives, regrets, and complicated histories — a structural choice that gives the writers a deeper emotional well to draw from. When something starts hunting them, the stakes feel different than they do in shows about high schoolers. These are people who have already survived a lot.

Who Made It?

The Boroughs is produced by Monkey Massacre Productions, with the Duffer Brothers serving as executive producers. Showrunner duties go to Jeffrey Addiss and Will Matthews, the writing duo behind Netflix's The Dark Crystal: Age of Resistance, who bring a strong fantasy-world-building pedigree.

The Duffer fingerprint matters here. Their producing track record — building tone, pacing slow-roll mystery, and orchestrating ensemble payoffs — is exactly what a show like this needs to land. Without that careful pacing, "creepy retirement community" risks becoming a one-joke premise. With it, you get something that could be a genuine prestige hit.

The Cast

Netflix has assembled a deliberately stacked, recognizable ensemble:

  • Alfred Molina as a former scientist hiding more than he lets on
  • Bill Camp, who anchors any drama he touches, as the unofficial leader of Wyndridge's investigators
  • Jeanine Serralles as a sharp-witted resident with a dark backstory
  • Jared Harris in a role Netflix has kept under tight wraps but trailers suggest is pivotal
  • Toks Olagundoye bringing comic relief and emotional grounding

Casting Molina, Camp, and Harris alone signals Netflix's confidence in this one. These are not actors who do filler genre work.

How Many Episodes and What's the Pacing?

Season 1 is eight episodes, all dropping May 21. Each runs between 50 and 60 minutes — closer to a streaming drama than to a tightly cut sitcom. That gives the show room to breathe and develop its ensemble, but it also means viewers expecting Stranger Things' kinetic action sequences may need to recalibrate. The Boroughs is being described as a slower, more atmospheric piece, with horror elements arriving gradually rather than upfront.

Is It Scary or More Mystery?

From the marketing, The Boroughs lands closer to The Haunting of Hill House on the horror spectrum than to a pure scare-fest like Midnight Mass. There is dread, there are unsettling images, and there is a clear horror premise underneath, but the show seems primarily interested in its characters and the central mystery. Expect a few well-placed jump moments rather than relentless terror.

If you loved the first season of Stranger Things specifically because of how it built atmosphere — the long shots of small-town life with something moving just out of frame — The Boroughs is aiming squarely at that wavelength.

Should You Watch It on Day One?

Three reasons to commit early:

  1. The cast. Molina, Camp, and Harris in the same ensemble is the kind of acting bench Netflix rarely builds for genre TV.
  2. The Duffer producing pedigree. They know how to construct a binge.
  3. The hook is genuinely fresh. Supernatural mystery starring seniors is not a premise you have seen ten times this year.

One reason to wait: if you generally bounce off slow-burn shows that take three or four episodes to get going, you might want to let early reviews land first. Eight 60-minute episodes is a real commitment.
