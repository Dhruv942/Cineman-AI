Wicked: For Good Streaming on Peacock — IMDb & RT Ratings Breakdown

Updated: March 31, 2026 11 min read

TL;DR

Wicked: For Good holds a 7.4 IMDb rating and 89% on Rotten Tomatoes. It is now streaming on Peacock and available for digital purchase. The Wicked movie phenomenon continues. After Part 1 dominated the 2024 holiday box office with over $680 million worldwide, its conclusion — Wicked: For Good — arrived in theaters and has now made the leap to streaming. The two-part adaptation of the beloved Broadway musical starring Cynthia Erivo as Elphaba and Ariana Grande as Glinda has been one of the most anticipated film events in years, and Part 2 carries the weight of resolving a story that audiences have been emotionally invested in since the stage show debuted over two decades ago.

Now that For Good is streaming on Peacock, here is the full ratings breakdown and our verdict on whether the conclusion lives up to the promise of Part 1.

Ratings: Part 1 vs. Part 2

Metric Wicked: Part 1 (2024) Wicked: For Good (2026)
IMDb Rating 7.6 7.4
RT Tomatometer 90% 89%
RT Audience Score 91% 88%
Box Office (Worldwide) $681M $595M
Runtime 160 min 150 min

The numbers reveal a sequel that comes very close to matching its predecessor but does not quite reach the same heights. The RT Tomatometer difference is marginal — 89% versus 90% is essentially a statistical tie — but the IMDb drop from 7.6 to 7.4 and the audience score dip from 91% to 88% suggest that general audiences found For Good slightly less satisfying than Part 1.

The box office decline is more significant on paper but is actually typical for second-part conclusions. The combined $1.27 billion for both films makes Wicked one of the most commercially successful musical adaptations in history, sitting alongside Les Miserables, The Greatest Showman, and the original Grease as a defining entry in the genre.

What Critics Are Saying

The critical consensus on For Good is that it delivers the emotional payoff that Part 1 set up, particularly in its final act. Cynthia Erivo's performance as Elphaba has been singled out as the film's greatest asset, with multiple critics calling her work in the climactic sequences among the best acting in any musical film. Ariana Grande continues to surprise as Glinda, bringing more depth to the character in Part 2 as the friendship between the two leads fractures under political pressure.

Where critics diverge is on the pacing. Part 1 had the advantage of being the setup — introducing characters, building the world, and establishing the emotional stakes. For Good has to deliver the resolution, and some reviewers feel that director Jon M. Chu takes too long getting there. The first hour is more deliberate than Part 1's equivalent stretch, and a few subplots feel expanded beyond what the story requires.

However, the final 40 minutes are widely praised as extraordinary. The titular musical number and its surrounding scenes deliver the emotional devastation that fans of the stage show were hoping for, and Chu's cinematic staging elevates the material beyond what a stage production can achieve.

The Musical Numbers

For Good contains some of the most beloved songs from the Broadway show, and the film's treatment of them has been a major talking point. Without going into specific details about which songs appear where (for those who want to experience them fresh), the consensus is that the musical numbers in Part 2 are more emotionally complex than Part 1's, trading the exuberance of the first film's showstoppers for something more bittersweet and layered.

The orchestral arrangements are bigger and more cinematic, and the sound mixing gives the vocals a presence that most musical films do not achieve. Both Erivo and Grande recorded their performances live on set for several key sequences, and the authenticity shows — there is a rawness to the vocal performances that studio recordings cannot replicate.

The Verdict

Wicked: For Good is a worthy conclusion to one of the most ambitious musical adaptations ever attempted. Its 7.4 IMDb and 89% RT scores reflect a film that is critically acclaimed and audience-approved, even if it does not quite match the novelty and energy of Part 1. Cynthia Erivo's performance alone justifies the price of a Peacock subscription, and the final act delivers the emotional catharsis that the two-film arc has been building toward. If you loved Part 1, For Good will not disappoint. If you are new to Wicked, start with Part 1 on Peacock and then let the story carry you through to its conclusion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I stream Wicked: For Good?

Wicked: For Good is streaming on Peacock in the United States. It is also available for digital purchase on platforms like Apple TV, Amazon Prime Video, and Google Play. International availability varies by region.

What is the IMDb rating for Wicked: For Good?

Wicked: For Good holds a 7.4 on IMDb, slightly below Part 1's 7.6. The Rotten Tomatoes score sits at 89%, reflecting strong critical reception for the sequel.

Do I need to watch Wicked Part 1 before For Good?

Yes. Wicked: For Good is a direct continuation of Part 1 and picks up exactly where the first film ended. Watching Part 1 first is essential for understanding the characters, relationships, and plot.

Is Wicked: For Good better than Part 1?

Critics are split. Part 1 has a slightly higher RT score (90% vs 89%) and IMDb rating (7.6 vs 7.4). However, many reviewers praise For Good's emotional depth and climax as more powerful than Part 1, even if the overall film is less consistently paced.
