Citadel Season 2 on Prime Video: Release Date, Cast & What to Expect (May 2026)

Updated: April 24, 2026 7 min read

TL;DR

Citadel Season 2 premieres on Prime Video on May 6, 2026. Richard Madden and Priyanka Chopra Jonas return. The Russo Brothers' spy franchise expands its scope with new international threads and tighter action. Worth watching if you enjoyed Season 1; production reports suggest it addresses the pacing issues that divided critics.

Citadel was one of the most expensive television productions in Amazon's history when it launched in 2023. The reaction was polarized: critics called it bloated and convoluted, while audiences responded with enough enthusiasm to generate over 25 million Prime Video viewers in its first month. That audience verdict was enough to guarantee Season 2, which arrives May 6, 2026 with the Russo Brothers promising a leaner, faster show.

Release Date: May 6, 2026

Citadel Season 2 premieres on Prime Video on May 6, 2026. Unlike Good Omens Season 3 (full season drop on May 13), Citadel Season 2 uses weekly episode releases — consistent with how Season 1 was released. Prime Video has been gradually shifting some of its prestige series to weekly formats to sustain engagement over a longer window and drive subscription retention.

The Cast

Richard Madden as Mason Kane

Richard Madden returns as Mason Kane, the former Citadel agent with wiped memory who spent Season 1 reconstructing his identity while fighting Manticore. Madden's physicality-forward performance was one of Season 1's clearest strengths. The action sequences built around Kane were among the most technically accomplished in recent streaming television. Season 2 is expected to give Kane a more complete mission structure from the outset, rather than building him back up from amnesia — which should translate to more dynamic early episodes.

Priyanka Chopra Jonas as Nadia Sinh

Priyanka Chopra Jonas returns as Nadia Sinh, whose relationship with Kane and complex allegiances drove much of Season 1's emotional tension. Chopra Jonas earned strong reviews for Season 1 despite the show's mixed critical reception overall. Season 2 is reported to develop Nadia's backstory more fully and give her more independent agency in the plot rather than positioning her primarily in relation to Kane.

New Cast

Season 2 introduces several new characters, consistent with the show's aim to expand the Citadel universe globally. Details on new cast members have been kept under embargo ahead of the May 6 premiere.

The Citadel Universe

Amazon's original ambition for Citadel was to create a multi-show espionage universe, with the main English-language show as the hub and international spinoffs as satellites. Citadel: Diana (Italy) and Citadel: Honey Bunny (India) both launched in 2024 and 2025 respectively, with Honey Bunny performing especially well for Prime Video globally.

Season 2 of the main Citadel series is expected to connect more explicitly to these spinoff timelines, bringing in elements and characters from the broader universe. For viewers who watched Honey Bunny, this creates additional narrative threads. For those who have only seen the main show, Season 2 is still designed to function as a standalone continuation.

What the Russo Brothers Changed for Season 2

Anthony and Joe Russo (Avengers: Infinity War, Endgame) serve as executive producers and directed key Season 2 episodes. Their public comments about the Season 1 reception were candid — they acknowledged that the criticism around pacing and plotting was valid and that they used the gap between seasons to restructure the story architecture.

Specifically, they have mentioned:

  • Reducing the number of simultaneous timelines. Season 1 juggled past and present versions of multiple characters, which several critics found disorienting. Season 2 streamlines this.
  • Front-loading the action. Season 1's most criticized stretch was its middle episodes, where the plot stalled during exposition-heavy sequences. Season 2 distributes action more evenly across the episode order.
  • Clearer villain motivation. Manticore in Season 1 was often described as a generic "evil organization." Season 2 develops the antagonists' specific goals and leadership more substantively.

Is Citadel Worth Watching If You Skipped Season 1?

You cannot start with Season 2. The show's mythology, character relationships, and ongoing plot require Season 1 context. The good news is that Season 1 is six episodes and moves quickly once the amnesia setup resolves — it is a manageable catch-up before May 6. If you were put off by critics in 2023, the audience reception was warmer than critical reviews suggested. Think of it as a big-budget popcorn spy thriller rather than nuanced prestige drama and it largely delivers.
