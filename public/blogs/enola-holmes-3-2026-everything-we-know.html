Enola Holmes 3 (2026): Millie Bobby Brown Goes to Malta

Published: May 15, 2026 9 min read

TL;DR

Enola Holmes 3 drops on Netflix July 1, 2026. Millie Bobby Brown returns as the teenage detective, with Henry Cavill back as Sherlock and a new mystery that takes Enola to Malta for the first time outside England. The Enola Holmes franchise has been one of Netflix's quieter wins. Two films, both Top 10 globally for weeks, both pulling above 75% on Rotten Tomatoes, both anchored by Millie Bobby Brown's reliably charming lead performance. The third film, releasing on July 1, 2026, is the first to push beyond Victorian London — and the first to feel like the franchise is willing to grow up alongside its star.

Plot: What We Know

Netflix's official synopsis: "Now an established detective, Enola is hired to investigate the disappearance of a wealthy heiress's fiance — a case that takes her from London to the sun-bleached streets of Valletta, Malta, and pulls her brother Sherlock into a darker mystery than either of them expected."

What that suggests:

  • Enola is no longer the underdog. She is running her own detective agency, with a reputation. The franchise is past the "young girl proves she can solve mysteries" arc.
  • The Sherlock-Enola dynamic is foregrounded. The film reportedly opens in London with the siblings working together before the case sends Enola abroad alone.
  • The Malta setting is functional, not just aesthetic — the case involves the colonial trade routes of the late 1880s.
  • Tewkesbury (Louis Partridge), Enola's romantic interest, is back, but the relationship is reportedly tested by the geographic distance.

Cast

  • Millie Bobby Brown as Enola Holmes
  • Henry Cavill as Sherlock Holmes (expanded role)
  • Louis Partridge as Lord Tewkesbury
  • Helena Bonham Carter as Eudoria Holmes (the matriarch, in flashback and present-day scenes)
  • Sharon Duncan-Brewster as a new character: a Maltese government official with her own agenda
  • Adeel Akhtar as Inspector Lestrade (returning)
  • David Thewlis in a villain role — reportedly the season's primary antagonist

Director and Creative Team

This is the franchise's biggest creative shift. Harry Bradbeer directed the first two films with a breezy, fourth-wall-breaking energy that matched Brown's performance. The third film hires Philip Barantini, fresh off the Adolescence Netflix miniseries that became one of 2025's most-watched shows.

Barantini is known for his single-take action sequences and a rougher, more grounded camera style. Expect the chase scenes to feel different. The whimsy will not entirely disappear — Brown's direct-address asides reportedly remain — but the texture shifts toward something closer to a real detective film.

Screenwriter Jack Thorne returns. The script reportedly draws elements from Nancy Springer's fifth Enola Holmes novel, The Case of the Cryptic Crinoline.

How It Compares to the First Two Films

For context, here is where the previous two land:

  • Enola Holmes (2020): 6.6 IMDb, 91% RT critics, 76% RT audience
  • Enola Holmes 2 (2022): 6.7 IMDb, 95% RT critics, 80% RT audience

The IMDb scores understate the franchise's appeal — Enola Holmes 2 was Netflix's #1 English-language film for three consecutive weeks at release. The audience is younger, more female-skewing, and more international than IMDb's user base. Should You Watch It?

If you liked the first two, this is a confident step forward. The Malta setting alone gives the franchise a visual reset that the formula needed. Brown is at this point genuinely gifted in the role and the writing finally treats Enola as the adult she has slowly grown into.

If you skipped the first two: you can technically start here, but the brother-sister dynamic and the Tewkesbury romance both have history that the third film assumes you know. Watch Enola Holmes 2 first — it is the strongest of the three.

Best paired with: a Sunday afternoon, a cup of tea, and zero patience for grim prestige drama.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does Enola Holmes 3 release on Netflix?

It releases worldwide on Netflix on July 1, 2026, with no theatrical window.

Is Henry Cavill returning as Sherlock Holmes?

Yes. Cavill's role is expanded compared to the first two films, with the Sherlock-Enola dynamic positioned as a central emotional arc.

Why is the film set in Malta?

The case takes Enola abroad for the first time. The plot involves the colonial trade routes of the 1880s, with Valletta as the central location.

Is this the last Enola Holmes film?

No. Netflix has not confirmed a fourth film, but Brown has said she is open to more, and there are multiple Nancy Springer source novels left to adapt.
