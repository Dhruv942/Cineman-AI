Good Omens Season 3 on Prime Video: Release Date, Cast & Full Guide (2026)

Updated: April 24, 2026 8 min read

TL;DR

Good Omens Season 3 drops on Prime Video on May 13, 2026. It is the confirmed final season. Michael Sheen and David Tennant return. The season resolves the cliffhanger from Season 2's divisive finale. Watch Seasons 1 and 2 on Prime Video before then if you haven't already.

Good Omens has had one of the more unusual trajectories in modern prestige television. The 2019 first season — adapted from Terry Pratchett and Neil Gaiman's beloved 1990 novel — was a critical and audience darling that exceeded expectations for a property many considered unfilmable. Season 2, which arrived in 2023, was original material written by Gaiman (Pratchett died in 2015) that extended the angel-and-demon partnership story. Its ending divided audiences sharply. Season 3, arriving May 13, 2026, promises to resolve everything.

Release Date and Format

Good Omens Season 3 premieres on Prime Video on May 13, 2026. Consistent with how Prime Video released Season 2, all episodes are expected to drop simultaneously as a full-season dump rather than weekly releases. This means you can watch the entire final season in one go from day one — or stretch it out however you prefer.

The full-season release format is appropriate for Good Omens, which is structured more like an extended film than a weekly procedural. Gaiman has said he thinks of the three seasons as acts of a single story, which makes binge-watching the natural viewing approach.

Cast: Who Returns for Season 3

Michael Sheen as Aziraphale

Michael Sheen's Aziraphale — the anxious, bookshop-owning angel who is rather too fond of Earth — is confirmed to return. Season 2 ended with Aziraphale accepting a position as the new Supreme Archangel, a decision that appeared to break his partnership with Crowley. Season 3 will deal with the aftermath of that choice. Sheen's portrayal of Aziraphale has been one of the most beloved character performances in recent British television, combining physical comedy with genuinely moving emotional vulnerability.

David Tennant as Crowley

David Tennant's Crowley — the demon-turned-reluctant-ally with a weakness for Bentleys, Queen, and his oldest friend — also returns. Tennant's Season 2 performance was widely considered even stronger than Season 1, and his final scene of that season (responding to Aziraphale's departure with "I forgive you") was emotionally devastating for many viewers. How Crowley navigates Aziraphale's absence will be central to Season 3's emotional architecture.

Supporting Cast

The broader ensemble from Seasons 1 and 2 is expected to return, including characters introduced in Season 2 who represent Heaven's new power structure. Details on returning cast members have been kept deliberately vague to avoid spoiling plot developments. Given the Season 2 cliffhanger involved significant supernatural politics, expect appearances from both angelic and demonic hierarchies.

What Happened at the End of Season 2

Season 2 ended with Aziraphale accepting Gabriel's former position as Supreme Archangel, believing he could change Heaven from within. He invited Crowley to come with him as the first demon in Heaven. Crowley refused, and in the final moments asked Aziraphale to leave Heaven and choose him instead. Aziraphale's failure to do so — and Crowley's response — was the scene that divided audiences. Many viewers felt it was a earned emotional gut-punch; others felt it felt out of character for Aziraphale given six episodes of development.

Season 3 picks up from this separation. The core tension: can the relationship between Crowley and Aziraphale survive Aziraphale's institutional choice, and what happens when Heaven's new Supreme Archangel discovers what Aziraphale has actually signed up for?

Neil Gaiman's Involvement

Good Omens has been centrally shaped by Neil Gaiman as showrunner and primary writer. Gaiman has faced significant personal and professional challenges in the period between Season 2 and Season 3, and the production timeline was affected by various factors. Despite this, Season 3 has proceeded to completion and is confirmed for the May 2026 release. Gaiman has described Season 3 as "the ending the story deserves" and has stated that he and Terry Pratchett had discussed how the Good Omens story should ultimately conclude.

Why Good Omens Season 3 Matters

Good Omens has always been about more than its comedy-fantasy surface. At its core, the show is about two beings who have chosen loyalty to each other over loyalty to the institutions they were created to serve. The central relationship between Aziraphale and Crowley is one of the most carefully developed partnerships in recent television, built across decades of shared history that the show gradually reveals through flashbacks and character moments.

Season 3 carries the weight of that setup. It is the season that either pays off the accumulated investment viewers have made across the previous two seasons and the novel, or it does not. That stakes-raising is what makes it among the most significant Prime Video events of 2026.

How Good Omens Compares to Prime Video's Other May 2026 Releases

Prime Video's May 2026 slate is unusually strong. Citadel Season 2 arrives May 6, and Spider-Noir on May 27. Good Omens Season 3 on May 13 is sandwiched between them. Of the three, Good Omens has the deepest existing fanbase and the highest emotional investment from returning viewers. Citadel Season 2 is likely to reach a broader audience. Spider-Noir is the wildcard. If you can only prioritize one Prime Video show in May, and you have seen Seasons 1 and 2 of Good Omens, Season 3 is the easy answer.

Where to Watch Good Omens Season 3

Good Omens Season 3 is a Prime Video exclusive. You need an Amazon Prime Video subscription to watch it. All episodes of Seasons 1 and 2 are also on Prime Video if you need to catch up before May 13. The show is not available on Netflix, Max, or Disney+.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does Good Omens Season 3 come out on Prime Video?

Yes. Both Michael Sheen as Aziraphale and David Tennant as Crowley return for the final season.
