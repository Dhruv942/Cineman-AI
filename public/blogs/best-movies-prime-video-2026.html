Best Prestige Drama Picks

Manchester by the Sea is Kenneth Lonergan's grief drama and is as emotionally precise as American cinema gets. Casey Affleck's performance is haunting. Not a "fun watch" but an essential one.

Sound of Metal with Riz Ahmed is the rare film that uses sound design as its thematic core. Won the Oscar for Best Sound. Ahmed's performance is a career-defining role.

The Holdovers, Alexander Payne's return to form, is currently on Prime and is the rare holiday-set film that works any time of year. Paul Giamatti is perfect.

Best Thrillers

The Handmaiden is Park Chan-wook's twisty Victorian-era lesbian heist drama, which sounds niche but is actually one of the most accessible entries in his catalog. 8.1 IMDb, 95 percent RT.

You Were Never Really Here with Joaquin Phoenix is Lynne Ramsay's bruising, economical thriller. 90 minutes of concentrated dread. Not for everyone, but a masterclass for those it lands with.

The Report, a political thriller about the Senate torture report, is one of the most underrated Prime originals. Adam Driver and Annette Bening are both excellent.

Best Comedy Picks

The Big Sick, Kumail Nanjiani and Emily V. Gordon's semi-autobiographical rom-com, remains the best romantic comedy of the last decade. 98 percent Rotten Tomatoes.

Coming to America is a legitimate Eddie Murphy classic and arguably the most rewatchable comedy on Prime. The Queens, New York world-building alone is worth the watch.

Jojo Rabbit is Taika Waititi's daring Holocaust dark comedy. It should not work; it does.

Best Popcorn Watches

The Tomorrow War is the most-watched Prime original of the last few years. It is not critically beloved but it is genuinely fun sci-fi action with Chris Pratt doing exactly what you want from a Chris Pratt action movie.

Road House (2024) with Jake Gyllenhaal is a divisive remake that landed better than expected. Low bar, but Doug Liman's take has moments.

Air is the Ben Affleck-directed Nike-Jordan deal drama, and it is as charming and effortlessly watchable as it sounds. 93 percent Rotten Tomatoes.

Best Indie Gems

My Old Ass is the sleeper hit of 2024, a time-travel coming-of-age film about a 39-year-old giving advice to her teenage self. 94 percent Rotten Tomatoes.

The Peanut Butter Falcon is the kind of small, warm independent film Prime Video has excelled at highlighting. Shia LaBeouf's best performance.

Licorice Pizza, Paul Thomas Anderson's 1970s San Fernando Valley coming-of-age, won't be everyone's favorite PTA but has a devoted following.

How We Ranked

Composite scoring: IMDb user score (40%), Rotten Tomatoes critics (35%), TMDB audience score (25%). Only films available with a standard Prime subscription (not rental) at time of writing were considered. We capped our selection at one film per director to maximize variety.

Don't Overlook Prime Original Shows

While this list focuses on movies, Prime Video has become as strong in television as it is in film. Invincible Season 4 is wrapping this month and The Boys Season 5 is in its final run. If you have Prime for movies, you should absolutely be using it for TV too.
