Where Season 4 Picks Up

Season 3 ended with Mark Grayson in his darkest place to date: he had accepted the Viltrumite heritage that Omni-Man spent years trying to pass down, and his decision to kill Angstrom Levy had rattled everyone around him, Eve and Debbie especially. The question Season 4 asks is: if Mark is on the Viltrumite path, where does that leave the earth he's supposed to protect, and the people who love him?

Season 4 also finally pulls the trigger on the Viltrumite War storyline that comic readers have been waiting on since Season 1. The scope is bigger, the stakes are higher, and the casualty count has already been eye-popping.

What Season 4 Is Actually About (Thematically)

Invincible has always been a show about inherited violence. Every season turns a screw on that theme. Season 4 is the first season to directly challenge whether Mark can meaningfully reject his father's legacy or whether the choice itself was always an illusion. The supporting cast also gets meatier material than usual: Eve's new arc is the best character work the show has ever done for her, and Debbie continues to be the moral spine of the entire series.

What to Expect from the Finale

We will keep this spoiler-free, but the trailer for Episode 8 points to a head-on confrontation with Thragg and a Mark decision that will define the rest of the series. Based on the pacing of the previous seven episodes, expect:

  • One major character death
  • A significant Omni-Man moment (Nolan Grayson's arc is a huge part of the season)
  • A cliffhanger that sets up Season 5's direction
  • An Atom Eve scene that will make viewers cry publicly

Season 5 Outlook

Prime Video officially renewed Invincible for Season 5 on the day Season 4's first three episodes dropped. No premiere date yet, but based on the show's typical production cycle, we are likely looking at late 2027 or early 2028. Robert Kirkman has said publicly that he sees the show running seven to eight seasons total and that Seasons 5 and 6 will cover the most ambitious arcs of the comic.

Why Invincible Is the Best Superhero Show on TV Right Now

With The Boys ending in May, Invincible is about to inherit the crown as the best adult superhero show on any streaming service. Its 96 percent Rotten Tomatoes score for Season 4 is higher than the final season of The Boys (98 percent but with half the reviews in), higher than Peacemaker Season 2, higher than Daredevil: Born Again Season 2, and higher than any MCU series currently on Disney+.

Its combination of violence, emotional stakes, voice performances (Steven Yeun, J.K. Simmons, Sandra Oh), and genuine surprise makes it one of the must-watch shows of the year.
