The Tier That Won’t Be Beaten

The first six entries on this list are essentially uncontested at the top. The Wire, Chernobyl, The Sopranos, Succession, Band of Brothers, and Game of Thrones (early seasons) are the consensus prestige TV canon — each one a foundational reference for everything that came after. If you have somehow not watched these and you have an HBO Max subscription, do not let another month pass without starting one.

The Wire

The institutional drama that defined the form. Five seasons, five different lenses on Baltimore (drugs, ports, schools, politics, media). Slow start, transformative payoff. The show critics call the best of all time more often than any other.

Chernobyl

Five episodes, no fat. The reconstruction of the 1986 nuclear disaster doubles as a study of institutional rot under pressure. Probably the most quotable miniseries of the last decade. Watch all five back-to-back if you can.

The Sopranos

Twenty-five years on, this is still the show modern prestige TV is in dialogue with. Tony Soprano is the prototype antihero. The cut-to-black ending generated a decade of debate. Holds up entirely on rewatch.

Succession

Four seasons of the worst people in America fighting over a media empire. Funnier than any drama has a right to be, sadder than any comedy. If you are looking for one prestige series that captures the present moment, this is it.

The Newer Prestige Hits

The Last of Us

The video-game adaptation that shouldn’t have worked. Pedro Pascal and Bella Ramsey carry a series that uses the post-pandemic world as a backdrop for a generational father-daughter story. Season 2 expanded the scope. Season 3 is in production.

The Penguin

Colin Farrell’s Oz Cobb — a side character from the 2022 Batman film — got a full eight-episode showcase that turned out to be one of the best dramas of the year. 95% Rotten Tomatoes. Ended its first season with a clear setup for season two.

Hacks

Jean Smart and Hannah Einbinder are one of TV’s best comic duos. The show has gotten sharper every season — an unusual trajectory in modern comedy. Currently sitting at 98% on Rotten Tomatoes across all four seasons.

The Best Limited Series

HBO Max’s limited-series pipeline is one of the streamer’s strongest assets. If you want to watch something excellent in a single sitting or weekend, these all qualify:

  • Chernobyl — 5 episodes
  • Mare of Easttown — 7 episodes
  • Watchmen — 9 episodes
  • Band of Brothers — 10 episodes (longer but contained)
  • The White Lotus Season 1 — 6 episodes

None of these has a season-two-and-beyond commitment behind it. You can finish the entire arc and feel done.

What’s Coming Next on HBO Max
