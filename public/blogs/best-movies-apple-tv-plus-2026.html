Apple TV+'s Strategy — And Why It Matters

Apple has explicitly chosen quality over volume. They release roughly 12 to 18 original films a year, compared to Netflix's 70-plus. Each one gets prestige marketing, top-tier directors, and theatrical runs designed for awards consideration. That commitment shows up in the average IMDb and RT scores: their slate's median film performs better critically than any other streamer's.

The downside, of course, is depth. If you finish your list of must-watch Apple TV+ films, the well runs dry faster than on Netflix. The strategy is built for subscribers who watch fewer films, more carefully — not for binge-everything weekenders.

The Three Categories Apple TV+ Excels At

Prestige Drama

CODA, Killers of the Flower Moon, Causeway, and Emancipation are the films Apple is built around — awards-track dramas with major directors and Oscar-eligible performances. Even when they miss commercially (Emancipation), they tend to deliver technically.

Smart Crowd-Pleasers

Tetris, Sharper, Spirited, and Flora and Son represent a category Apple does better than expected: middlebrow films with broad appeal that still respect the audience. These are the closest equivalents to mid-budget studio releases of the 1990s.

Indie Discoveries

Cha Cha Real Smooth, Causeway, Fingernails — Apple has been quietly buying up Sundance and TIFF titles that would otherwise vanish. These are exactly the kind of films you would never find through Netflix's algorithm.

What's Missing from the Catalog

Apple TV+ is light on action blockbusters, horror, and pure comedy. There is essentially no Apple TV+ equivalent to John Wick or The Conjuring. If those genres are your primary draw, you will need a second subscription. But for drama, prestige biopic, indie discovery, and the occasional musical — this is the platform.
