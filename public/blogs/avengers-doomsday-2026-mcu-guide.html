Avengers: Doomsday (2026) — Release Date, Cast & MCU Phase 6 Guide

Updated: March 31, 2026 12 min read

TL;DR

Avengers: Doomsday arrives May 1, 2026, directed by the Russo Brothers, with Robert Downey Jr. returning as Doctor Doom. It is the MCU’s biggest crossover since Endgame. Below you will find the confirmed cast, an MCU rewatch order, every MCU film ranked by IMDb, and how CineMan AI helps you navigate the sprawling MCU catalog.

The Marvel Cinematic Universe has been building toward something massive since Avengers: Endgame closed the Infinity Saga in 2019. After a Phase 4 that expanded the multiverse and a Phase 5 that introduced new heroes and threats, everything converges in Avengers: Doomsday. This is the film that Marvel Studios hopes will recapture the lightning-in-a-bottle energy of Infinity War and Endgame, and they have made one of the boldest casting decisions in blockbuster history to do it: bringing Robert Downey Jr. back — not as Tony Stark, but as the MCU’s new ultimate villain, Doctor Doom.

Here is everything confirmed about Avengers: Doomsday, the optimal rewatch order for the MCU, and how every Marvel film stacks up by audience ratings.

Everything Confirmed About Avengers: Doomsday

Avengers: Doomsday is scheduled for release on May 1, 2026. Joe and Anthony Russo are returning to direct after helming Captain America: The Winter Soldier, Captain America: Civil War, Avengers: Infinity War, and Avengers: Endgame — four of the highest-grossing and best-reviewed MCU films. Their return signals that Marvel Studios is treating Doomsday with the same level of importance as the Infinity Saga finale.

The film will serve as the culmination of the Multiverse Saga, which began with Spider-Man: No Way Home and Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness. Doctor Doom, played by Robert Downey Jr., is expected to be a multiversal variant — a version of the classic Marvel villain from an alternate timeline. This allows Downey to play a completely different character while leveraging the audience’s deep emotional connection to his presence in the MCU.

The Cast: Who Is Returning?

The full ensemble has not been officially confirmed, but here is what we know:

  • Robert Downey Jr. as Doctor Doom — The centerpiece casting. Downey is not reprising Tony Stark but inhabiting a new, villainous role.
  • Benedict Cumberbatch as Doctor Strange — As the character most tied to the multiverse storyline, Strange is expected to play a central role.
  • Tom Holland as Spider-Man — Holland has confirmed involvement in the next Avengers cycle.
  • Chris Hemsworth as Thor — The God of Thunder remains one of the few original Avengers still active in the MCU.
  • Simu Liu as Shang-Chi — One of the strongest Phase 4 additions.
  • Florence Pugh as Yelena Belova — Taking on a larger role in the post-Endgame MCU.
  • Pedro Pascal as Reed Richards / Mister Fantastic — Introduced in The Fantastic Four: First Steps.

Expect additional casting announcements throughout 2026. The Russo Brothers’ previous Avengers films featured nearly every active MCU hero, and Doomsday is expected to follow that tradition.

MCU Rewatch Order Before Doomsday

With over 30 films in the MCU, a complete rewatch is a serious time commitment. Here is a focused rewatch order that covers the essential narrative threads leading into Doomsday:

  1. Avengers: Infinity War (2018) — The template for what Doomsday aims to be.
  2. Avengers: Endgame (2019) — The conclusion of the Infinity Saga and the starting point for the multiverse.
  3. Spider-Man: No Way Home (2021) — The film that cracked the multiverse wide open.
  4. Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness (2022) — Deepens multiverse lore and introduces key concepts.
  5. The Fantastic Four: First Steps (2025) — Introduces Doctor Doom’s world and Reed Richards.
  6. Thunderbolts* (2025) — Sets up the new team dynamics heading into Doomsday.

Why Doomsday Could Restore MCU Momentum

The MCU has faced criticism during Phases 4 and 5 for uneven quality, franchise fatigue, and a lack of clear direction. Films like Eternals (6.3 IMDb), Thor: Love and Thunder (6.3), and Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania (6.0) received mixed reception. The Disney+ series, while occasionally excellent, diluted the event-level feeling that made Infinity War and Endgame cultural phenomena.

Doomsday is Marvel’s answer to all of that. By bringing back the Russo Brothers, casting Robert Downey Jr. in a role that demands attention, and positioning it as the first Avengers film in seven years, Marvel is stacking every advantage they have. The question is whether audiences who drifted away during the multiverse era will return for the payoff.

The early indicators are promising. The Fantastic Four: First Steps is building positive buzz, and the Russo Brothers have publicly stated that Doomsday will be more focused and character-driven than the sprawling multiverse narratives of Phase 4. If they deliver, this could be the course correction the MCU needs.
