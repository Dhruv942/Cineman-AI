Toy Story 5 (2026): Pixar Takes On the Tablet

Published: May 15, 2026 10 min read

TL;DR

Toy Story 5 hits theaters June 19, 2026. Tom Hanks and Tim Allen return as Woody and Buzz. The premise: Bonnie has aged out of toys and is now obsessed with a tablet computer that the gang must somehow contend with. It is the most thematically pointed Toy Story since 3, with Pixar taking on the screen-time question parents have been asking for two decades. Toy Story 4 ended on a note that felt final. Woody chose Bo Peep over Bonnie. The toys watched their old friend ride into the sunset on the back of an antique-store shop window. It was as close to "the end" as a billion-dollar Pixar franchise was ever going to get.

Six years later, that ending is being reopened. Toy Story 5 releases in theaters on June 19, 2026, and Pixar's pitch is more interesting than the usual reboot logic. The toys are not facing a new villain. They are facing a tablet.

Plot: What We Know

The official Pixar logline: "When Bonnie's attention is captured by Lily, a sleek new electronic tablet, Buzz, Jessie, and the gang must figure out their place in a child's life that no longer includes them — and reach out to an old friend living far away for help."

What the trailer and early press have revealed:

  • Bonnie is now in third grade. She no longer plays with the toys.
  • "Lily" the tablet is voiced by Greta Lee. It is presented less as a villain and more as a competitor — charming, attentive, and impossible for analog toys to compete with.
  • Buzz takes the leadership role Woody held for the first three films, but he is reportedly out of his depth.
  • Jessie sends a distress signal that reaches Bo Peep and Woody, setting up a reunion.
  • The third act takes the toys outside Bonnie's house in a way the franchise has not done before — the trailer shows a sequence in what appears to be a children's hospital, where the toys make their case for why kids still need them.

Cast

The voice cast is the most complete reunion since Toy Story 3:

  • Tom Hanks as Woody
  • Tim Allen as Buzz Lightyear
  • Annie Potts as Bo Peep
  • Joan Cusack as Jessie
  • Tony Hale as Forky
  • Wallace Shawn as Rex
  • John Ratzenberger as Hamm (his contractually-mandatory Pixar cameo, properly weight here)
  • Greta Lee as Lily, the tablet
  • Conan O'Brien in a supporting role as a new toy character
  • Madeleine McGraw as Bonnie (the same actor who voiced her in Toy Story 4, now older)

Director and Creative Team

Andrew Stanton (Finding Nemo, WALL-E) directs — his first Toy Story credit and his first feature as solo director since Finding Dory. Stanton co-wrote Toy Story 1, 2, and 3, so he is not a newcomer to the franchise, but the directing chair brings a different perspective than Pete Docter or Lee Unkrich.

The thematic territory plays directly to Stanton's strengths. WALL-E was Pixar's first explicit critique of consumer technology and screen culture. Toy Story 5 looks like a sequel to that conversation as much as it is a sequel to Toy Story 4.

The Screen-Time Premise: A Risk Worth Taking?

This is the part that has Pixar fans nervous. "The villain is a tablet" reads on paper as a deeply reductive premise — the kind of thing that could date the film badly within five years. Tablets are not new. Children's relationship with screens is more complicated than "good toys, bad screens."

Early footage suggests the film knows this. Lily the tablet is presented sympathetically. She genuinely entertains Bonnie. She is not malicious. The conflict is less "evil tech vs. good toys" and more "what happens to old forms of play when new ones arrive." That is a more honest version of the question, and it is recognizably the territory Pixar usually does well.

The risk is execution. If the third act flattens into "tablets bad, imagination good," the film will feel preachy. If it lands the more nuanced version, this could be the most thematically interesting Toy Story since 3.

Should You Watch It?

It is Toy Story. If you have a child, it is mandatory. If you do not, the franchise's track record speaks for itself — this has been one of the most consistent runs in any medium for thirty years.

The more interesting question is whether Toy Story 5 finds anything new to say. Toy Story 4 was a beautiful film that did not really need to exist. Toy Story 5 has a thesis. Whether Pixar can land it is the only question that matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does Toy Story 5 release in theaters?

It releases worldwide in theaters on June 19, 2026, with a Disney+ release expected approximately 100 days later.

Are Tom Hanks and Tim Allen returning?

Yes. Tom Hanks reprises Woody and Tim Allen reprises Buzz Lightyear. Most of the original cast is back.

Do I need to watch Toy Story 4 first?

Strongly recommended. Toy Story 5 is a direct continuation of Toy Story 4's ending, with Woody having left Bonnie's room to live as a "lost toy" with Bo Peep.

What is Toy Story 5 actually about?

The toys face a new challenge: a tablet computer that captures Bonnie's attention. The film explores what it means to be a toy in an era when children play differently.
