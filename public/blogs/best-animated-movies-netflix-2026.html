Best for Family Movie Night

The Wild Robot is the easy answer in 2026. Chris Sanders (Lilo & Stitch, How to Train Your Dragon) directs an adaptation of Peter Brown's book about a robot learning to survive on a wild island. The animation has a painterly quality that belongs in the pantheon next to Studio Ghibli at its best. It made kids and adults cry in equal measure in theaters and plays even better at home.

The Mitchells vs. the Machines is the funniest family movie on Netflix, full stop. A dysfunctional road-trip family accidentally saves humanity from an AI uprising. Sony Pictures Animation working with Phil Lord and Chris Miller means the visual gags never stop. 97 percent on Rotten Tomatoes and deserved.

Klaus, Sergio Pablos's original Netflix animated film from 2019, remains the best holiday movie the service has produced. Its hand-drawn-with-3D-lighting look was genuinely innovative.

Best for Older Kids and Teens

KPop Demon Hunters is the animated breakout of the past year. Maggie Kang and Chris Appelhans direct a story about a K-pop girl group who secretly hunt demons between concerts. The musical numbers have become TikTok staples. 95 percent Rotten Tomatoes, Academy Award nomination, and you will not get the title track out of your head.

Nimona is a queer shapeshifter story adapted from ND Stevenson's graphic novel. Originally a Blue Sky Studios project that Disney shelved, Netflix rescued it, and it ended up as one of 2023's most acclaimed animated films. 94 percent critical score.

Guillermo del Toro's Pinocchio is the Oscar winner for Best Animated Feature in 2022. Stop-motion, dark, emotionally raw, and frequently beautiful. Not for very young children.

Best for Adults Watching Alone

Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse is one of the most formally ambitious animated films of the 2020s and probably the best comic-book movie of its decade. 95 percent RT, 8.5 IMDb, and it somehow holds up after multiple rewatches.

I Lost My Body is a French animated film about a severed hand crawling across Paris to find the man it belongs to. It sounds absurd; it is devastating. 96 percent Rotten Tomatoes and an Annecy Grand Prix winner.

Spirited Away and My Neighbor Totoro are Netflix's biggest Ghibli additions (Japan has them; U.S. regional availability varies). If you have not seen either, 2026 is a fine year to fix that.

Best for Very Young Kids

Leo is Adam Sandler's animated musical for Netflix, aimed squarely at elementary-school kids. It is not going to change your life, but it is genuinely pleasant, and kids love it. 86 percent RT.

Over the Moon is the visually stunning Netflix animated musical with Chinese folklore roots. Songs are hit-or-miss; the animation is consistently gorgeous.

How We Ranked These

Same composite method as our other genre rankings: IMDb user score (40%), Rotten Tomatoes critic score (35%), TMDB audience score (25%). This approach helps cut through the cases where critic and audience opinion diverge sharply — rare in animation, but it happens.
