Indian cinema is not just Bollywood. That is the first and most important thing to understand about the current state of Indian filmmaking. While Hindi-language Bollywood remains the most commercially visible segment, some of the most critically acclaimed and innovative Indian films of the past decade have come from Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and Kannada cinema. Streaming platforms have made all of this accessible to global audiences in a way that was impossible even five years ago.

The challenge is discovery. Indian streaming platforms like JioHotstar and JioCinema have enormous libraries but poor curation. Netflix and Prime Video carry selected Indian titles but bury them beneath their Western-focused algorithms. Without quality signals, finding the gems among hundreds of titles is genuinely difficult. This guide organizes the best Indian films currently streaming by language, with IMDb and Rotten Tomatoes ratings for every pick.

12th Fail (IMDb 8.8, RT 90%) became a phenomenon in late 2023 and continues to be one of the highest-rated Indian films on IMDb. Based on the true story of a young man from a small village preparing for India's notoriously difficult civil service exams, it is an underdog story that avoids every cliche you expect from the genre. The emotional restraint is what makes it hit so hard — it earns every moment instead of manipulating you into feeling something.

Tumbbad (IMDb 8.2, RT 86%) is unlike anything else in Indian cinema. Set in a rain-drenched village in colonial-era India, it tells the story of a man who discovers a mythological creature guarding treasure beneath an old mansion. The film is a gothic horror-fantasy that draws on Indian mythology rather than Western horror tropes, and the result is genuinely unsettling. The production design is extraordinary, and the creature effects are some of the best in any Indian film.

Andhadhun (IMDb 8.2, RT 100%) is a pitch-perfect dark comedy thriller about a pianist who pretends to be blind and accidentally witnesses a murder. Director Sriram Raghavan constructs a plot with more twists than a corkscrew, and every single one lands. The 100% Rotten Tomatoes score is richly deserved — this is smart, stylish filmmaking that competes with the best thrillers from any country.

Gangs of Wasseypur (IMDb 8.2, RT 96%) is Anurag Kashyap's epic crime saga spanning three generations of a coal-mining family's criminal empire. At over five hours across two parts, it is an immense commitment, but the storytelling is so propulsive and the characters so vivid that the runtime flies. Think of it as India's answer to the great American gangster epics, but with a distinctly subcontinental energy.

Article 15 (IMDb 7.8, RT 88%) is a socially conscious thriller about a newly appointed police officer investigating caste-based crimes in rural India. Ayushmann Khurrana delivers a restrained, powerful performance, and the film tackles India's caste system with unflinching honesty. It is gripping as a procedural and important as social commentary.

Panchayat (IMDb 8.9) deserves special mention as one of the best Indian series on any platform. An engineering graduate reluctantly takes a job as secretary of a remote village council. What sounds like a simple premise becomes a warm, funny, and surprisingly touching exploration of rural Indian life. Three seasons in, it has only gotten better.

Drishyam (IMDb 8.2, RT 84%) is a masterclass in suspense. A cable TV repairman uses his encyclopedic knowledge of crime movies to cover up an accidental killing and protect his family from a relentless police investigation. The cat-and-mouse game between the protagonist and the investigating officer is riveting, and the final reveal is immensely satisfying.

Lunchbox (IMDb 7.8, RT 97%) is a gentle, melancholic romance about two lonely people who begin exchanging letters through Mumbai's famous dabbawala lunch delivery system. Irrfan Khan's quietly devastating performance anchors a film that captures loneliness in a crowded city with extraordinary tenderness.

Vikram (IMDb 7.4, RT 90%) is Lokesh Kanagaraj's action-thriller that brings together three generations of Tamil superstars — Kamal Haasan, Vijay Sethupathi, and Fahadh Faasil. A retired agent is pulled back into the world of black ops when his son is killed. The action is explosive, the plot is layered with connections to Kanagaraj's broader cinematic universe, and the performances are magnetic.

Jai Bhim (IMDb 8.7, RT 89%) tells the real story of a lawyer fighting for a tribal man who was illegally arrested and disappeared by police. Suriya's performance is powerful, and the film's depiction of systemic injustice against marginalized communities is both enraging and deeply moving. It briefly became the highest-rated Indian film on IMDb upon release.

Super Deluxe (IMDb 8.1, RT 86%) weaves four seemingly unrelated stories together in a way that is funny, shocking, philosophical, and ultimately profound. The less you know going in, the better — each storyline subverts your expectations in surprising ways.

Asuran (IMDb 8.0, RT 83%) is a revenge drama about a father protecting his son from a powerful landlord. Dhanush delivers a dual performance as both a restrained older man and a fierce younger version of himself. The film's meditation on caste violence and generational trauma is deeply felt.

Soorarai Pottru (IMDb 8.3, RT 88%) is inspired by the real story of Air Deccan founder Captain Gopinath, who launched India's first budget airline. Suriya brings infectious energy to the role, and the film captures both the thrill of entrepreneurship and the systemic barriers that stand in the way of disruption.

RRR (IMDb 7.8, RT 95%) is the film that changed how the world sees Indian cinema. S.S. Rajamouli's maximalist action epic about two real-life Indian revolutionaries fighting the British Empire is the most exhilarating film experience of the decade. The action sequences are jaw-dropping, the bromance between the two leads is deeply felt, and the film's unabashed sincerity is infectious. It won an Oscar for Best Original Song and became a genuine global phenomenon.

Baahubali: The Beginning (IMDb 8.0, RT 86%) is the epic that launched India's blockbuster ambitions into a new era. Rajamouli's fantasy war saga has a scale and ambition that rivals Hollywood's biggest productions, with world-building that draws on Indian mythology and history.

C/o Kancharapalem (IMDb 8.2) is the quiet counterpoint to Telugu cinema's blockbuster reputation. Four love stories set in a small neighborhood, told with non-professional actors and documentary-like realism. It is gentle, observant, and deeply humane — proof that Telugu cinema encompasses far more than spectacle.

Hi Nanna (IMDb 7.8, RT 84%) is a family drama about a single father whose life is upended when his daughter discovers she has a mother. Nani's warm performance anchors a film that balances humor, romance, and genuine emotional depth without ever becoming manipulative.

Jallikattu (IMDb 7.0, RT 100%) was India's official Oscar submission and it is an extraordinary piece of cinema. When a buffalo escapes from a slaughterhouse in a small Kerala village, the entire community mobilizes to catch it. What starts as an adventure becomes a primal allegory about humanity's barely suppressed savagery. The 100% Rotten Tomatoes score reflects the critical consensus that this is a masterwork of pure filmmaking.

Kumbalangi Nights (IMDb 8.5, RT 94%) is one of the best Indian films of the past decade. Four dysfunctional brothers in a Kerala fishing village navigate love, family, and an increasingly terrifying brother-in-law. The film is simultaneously a warm family comedy, a tender romance, and a chilling psychological portrait. Fahadh Faasil's performance as the controlling Shammi is one of the most unsettling villain turns in Indian cinema.

The Great Indian Kitchen (IMDb 8.2, RT 90%) is a devastating portrait of a newlywed woman slowly suffocated by the domestic expectations of her conservative husband's household. The film uses the repetitive drudgery of kitchen work as a visual metaphor for patriarchal oppression. It is uncomfortable, deliberate, and necessary — a film that changed conversations in Indian households.

Minnal Murali (IMDb 7.6, RT 83%) is India's first genuinely good superhero film. Set in a small Kerala town in the 1990s, a tailor gains superpowers after being struck by lightning. But so does someone else. The film takes its small-town setting seriously, building a superhero origin story that is rooted in local culture rather than imitating Western formulas.

Manjummel Boys (IMDb 8.4, RT 88%) became a massive box office hit in 2024, crossing language barriers to become one of the year's biggest Indian films. Based on a true incident where a group of friends became trapped in an underground cave during a trip, the film is a heart-pounding survival thriller with an irresistible camaraderie among its ensemble cast.

Platform Comparison for Indian Viewers

Indian viewers face a fragmented streaming landscape. Here is how the major platforms compare for Indian content:

  • JioHotstar — The largest Indian content library, combining the former Disney+ Hotstar catalog with Jio's own acquisitions. Best for Hindi series, cricket, and mainstream Bollywood. Carries a strong selection across all Indian languages.
  • Prime Video India — Excellent for premium Indian originals (Panchayat, Mirzapur, The Family Man) and has the best Malayalam content selection among international platforms. Good balance of mainstream and independent films.
  • Netflix India — Smaller Indian library but higher average quality. Strong Netflix Original Indian content and the best interface for discovery. Has expanded its South Indian and regional language offerings significantly.
  • JioCinema — Growing library with a mix of older Bollywood catalog titles and newer additions. Competitive pricing makes it an attractive secondary platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best Bollywood movies on Netflix right now?

Some of the highest-rated Hindi films on Netflix include Andhadhun (IMDb 8.2, RT 100%), Article 15 (IMDb 7.8), and Lunchbox (IMDb 7.8, RT 97%). Netflix India also has a strong lineup of Netflix Original Hindi films and a growing selection of Tamil and Telugu titles.

Which streaming platform is best for Indian movies?

For sheer volume, JioHotstar and Prime Video India have the largest libraries. Netflix India has a smaller but more curated selection with higher average quality. The best choice depends on your language preference and whether you prioritize newer releases or catalog depth.

Are South Indian movies better than Bollywood?

Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and Kannada cinema have been producing consistently innovative and critically acclaimed films. Movies like RRR, Vikram, and the Malayalam new wave have gained international recognition.
