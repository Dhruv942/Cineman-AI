Hulu’s Hidden Strengths

Three categories where Hulu quietly out-punches its weight:

Searchlight and A24 Indies

Hulu carries most Searchlight Pictures titles within months of theatrical release — The Banshees of Inisherin, The Holdovers, Poor Things, Nomadland, and many more. Many A24 titles also land on Hulu (versus Max or Apple TV+) within a year of theatrical release.

FX Original Films

Some of the platform’s most acclaimed work bypasses theatrical entirely. The Patient, Justified, and the FX-produced film slate (often co-distributed with Searchlight) all live exclusively on Hulu.

Best Picture Nominees

Roughly 60-70% of recent Best Picture nominees have ended up on Hulu within a year of release, thanks to the Searchlight and 20th Century pipelines. That makes Hulu the most efficient platform for staying caught up on awards-season movies without juggling multiple subscriptions.

What Hulu Lacks

The pre-1990s catalog is thin compared to Max. Pure blockbuster action is also limited — Marvel and DC live elsewhere, and Hulu has very few of the year’s biggest tentpoles. If you want comic-book films and classic Hollywood, Hulu is not your primary platform.
