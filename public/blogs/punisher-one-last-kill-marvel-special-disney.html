The Punisher: One Last Kill — Marvel’s Disney+ Special Preview (2026)

Updated: May 9, 2026 9 min read

TL;DR

Marvel is bringing Jon Bernthal’s Frank Castle back in a TV-MA Special Presentation called The Punisher: One Last Kill, dropping on Disney+ in 2026. Roughly 50–60 minutes, full R-rated tone, set after his appearance in Daredevil: Born Again. The biggest signal yet that Marvel’s Disney+ slate is getting more violent and more grown-up. For most of the last decade the conventional wisdom was that the Punisher could not exist on Disney+ — that Disney corporate would never tolerate the level of violence Frank Castle requires to feel like Frank Castle. That conventional wisdom was wrong. Daredevil: Born Again already pushed the tone harder than anything else on the platform. Now Marvel is taking the next step: a dedicated, R-rated Punisher Special Presentation called One Last Kill.

Here is what we know, what we can reasonably guess, and why this project matters more than its 60-minute runtime suggests.

What Is a Marvel Special Presentation?

Special Presentations are Marvel’s shorter-form Disney+ category, sitting between an episode of TV and a short film. They run roughly 50 to 60 minutes. Previous entries include Werewolf by Night (2022) and The Guardians of the Galaxy Holiday Special (2022). The format is designed for self-contained character vehicles that do not need an eight-episode commitment.

Putting Frank Castle in this format is smart. It lets Marvel deliver an R-rated Punisher hit without the brand exposure of a full series, and without the budget pressure of a feature film. If it works, it scales. If it does not, the bruise is small.

Bernthal’s Frank Castle: A Quick Recap

Jon Bernthal first played Frank Castle in Daredevil Season 2 on Netflix in 2016. The performance was instantly definitive — widely considered the best on-screen Punisher across all live-action attempts. He carried two seasons of the standalone Punisher series on Netflix (2017–2019) before the original Marvel Television deal collapsed. Then he returned, finally, in Daredevil: Born Again, in scenes that felt like a deliberate handoff to whatever comes next.

One Last Kill is what comes next.

What We Know About the Plot

Marvel has been characteristically tight on plot specifics, but several things are confirmed or strongly implied:

  • The story takes place after the events of Daredevil: Born Again.
  • Castle is operating outside any institution — not embedded with police, military, or vigilante allies. Solo.
  • The “One Last Kill” framing suggests a job he believes will be his final one. Whether that is true or aspirational on his part is the dramatic engine.
  • Expect a contained, single-target story with a small supporting cast rather than a sprawling ensemble.

The closest tonal comparison is probably Logan — not in plot or scope, but in the willingness to treat a long-running character’s violence as something the character himself is finally exhausted by.

Why This Special Presentation Matters

1. Disney+ is finally allowing R-rated Marvel.

For years Disney+ insisted on TV-14 ceilings for Marvel content. Daredevil: Born Again broke that, and One Last Kill is going to break it harder. This is the platform’s public commitment to TV-MA Marvel as a category, not an exception.

2. It tests an audience theory.

Marvel has been worried for years about the gap between general-audience family viewing on Disney+ and the more mature fan base that drove Deadpool & Wolverine’s billion-dollar opening. One Last Kill is a low-risk, high-signal test of whether the same R-rated audience will watch on Disney+ at scale.

3. It positions Frank Castle for the next decade.

If One Last Kill lands, expect a full Punisher series order on Disney+ within twelve months. If it does not, expect Bernthal’s Frank Castle to settle into a recurring guest role across other Marvel street-level shows.

How It Connects to the Broader MCU

The Disney+ MCU is consolidating around its street-level corner. Daredevil: Born Again is the flagship. Echo opened a different door. Spider-Man: Brand New Day is reportedly being written to acknowledge the same New York neighborhoods. The Punisher fits cleanly into this ecosystem — he can plausibly cross paths with Daredevil, Spider-Man, or even Echo without rewriting anyone’s tone.

This is Phase Six MCU strategy in miniature: less interconnected event-tier crossover, more well-built corners that occasionally touch.

Should You Watch?

Watch it if:

  • You loved Bernthal’s Punisher on Netflix or in Born Again
  • You want a one-sit, contained R-rated Marvel watch
  • You enjoy the late-period grim character study (Logan, The Last of Us finale, True Detective)
  • You are a Disney+ subscriber wondering what to queue up after Daredevil: Born Again

Skip it if:

  • R-rated violence is a hard pass for you
  • You bounced off Daredevil: Born Again’s tonal direction

Bottom Line

One Last Kill is a small project with outsized strategic weight. It is Marvel’s clearest signal yet that the Disney+ MCU has a permanent grown-up wing. If you have been waiting for Bernthal’s Punisher to come back without a premium-cable detour, this is it.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does The Punisher: One Last Kill release?

Marvel has confirmed One Last Kill as part of its 2026 Disney+ Special Presentation slate. The exact date is being staggered closer to launch, but it is on the Marvel/Disney+ calendar for this year.

Is Jon Bernthal back as the Punisher?

Yes. Bernthal reprises Frank Castle. This is his fourth distinct Marvel project in the role after Daredevil (Netflix), the Punisher Netflix series, and Daredevil: Born Again.

Is the Punisher Special Presentation rated R?

Effectively yes. It is rated TV-MA on Disney+, which is the platform’s equivalent for an R-rated tone — in line with Daredevil: Born Again.

How long is The Punisher: One Last Kill?

Marvel Special Presentations run roughly 50 to 60 minutes. One Last Kill is expected to land in that range, similar in length to Werewolf by Night.
