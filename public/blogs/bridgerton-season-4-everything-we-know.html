Bridgerton Season 4: Release Date, Cast, Ratings & What to Expect

Bridgerton has become one of Netflix's most reliable franchises. Since its debut in December 2020, the Shonda Rhimes-produced period romance has generated enormous viewership numbers, a dedicated fanbase, and the kind of cultural conversation that most streaming shows can only dream of. Now, with Season 4 confirmed and in production, anticipation is building for the next chapter in the Bridgerton family saga.

Here is everything we know about Bridgerton Season 4 — the release window, the cast, the source material, how previous seasons have performed by the numbers, and what to watch while you wait.

Release Date and Production Timeline

Netflix officially renewed Bridgerton through Season 4 back in 2021, so its existence has never been in doubt. Production on Season 4 began in mid-2025, and filming has been taking place at the show's traditional UK locations, including the estates and studios in and around London and Bath.

Netflix has not confirmed an exact premiere date, but based on the production timeline and the franchise's history, a late 2026 release is the most likely scenario. Season 1 premiered in December 2020, Season 2 in March 2022, and Season 3 was split across May and June 2024. Given that filming wrapped in early 2026, post-production would need several months, pointing to a fall or winter 2026 premiere.

We will update this page as soon as Netflix makes an official announcement.

Whose Story Is Season 4?

Each Bridgerton season centers on a different sibling's love story, loosely adapted from Julia Quinn's book series. Season 1 covered Daphne (Phoebe Dynevor), Season 2 focused on Anthony (Jonathan Bailey), and Season 3 gave us Colin and Penelope's story (Luke Newton and Nicola Coughlan).

Season 4 will be Benedict Bridgerton's turn, played by Luke Thompson. The season is based on Quinn's third novel, which tells a Cinderella-inspired story of Benedict falling for a mysterious woman he meets at a masquerade ball, only to spend the season trying to find her again. The source material is widely considered one of the strongest books in Quinn's series, and fans have been eagerly waiting for Benedict's arc since Season 1.

Yerin Ha has been cast as Sophie Baek, the female lead opposite Thompson. The casting continues Bridgerton's approach to inclusive, color-conscious casting, and early reactions to the pairing have been enthusiastic.

Returning and New Cast Members

In addition to Luke Thompson as Benedict, Season 4 is expected to feature the return of several series regulars:

  • Luke Thompson as Benedict Bridgerton (lead)
  • Yerin Ha as Sophie Baek (new, female lead)
  • Nicola Coughlan as Penelope Featherington
  • Claudia Jessie as Eloise Bridgerton
  • Ruth Gemmell as Violet Bridgerton
  • Adjoa Andoh as Lady Danbury
  • Julie Andrews as Lady Whistledown (voice)

Whether Jonathan Bailey and Simone Ashley will return in significant roles remains unconfirmed, though the show has a pattern of giving previous leads cameo or supporting appearances in subsequent seasons.

The ratings tell an interesting story. There is a clear downward trend in both critical and audience scores across the three seasons, yet the viewership numbers remain remarkably stable. Bridgerton Season 3 was watched by 91 million households in its first 28 days despite lower reviews, suggesting that the show's audience is loyal enough to return regardless of individual season quality.

Season 1 remains the critical high point, with an 89% Rotten Tomatoes score and a 7.3 on IMDb. The novelty factor played a role — Bridgerton was genuinely unlike anything else on Netflix when it debuted — but the Daphne-Simon storyline also landed with audiences in a way that subsequent pairings have not quite replicated. Season 2's Anthony-Kate dynamic was well-received by many fans but did not achieve the same widespread enthusiasm. Season 3 divided audiences more sharply, with the Colin-Penelope pairing drawing mixed responses.

The question for Season 4 is whether Benedict's storyline can reverse the trend. The source material is strong, the Cinderella framework gives the season a clear narrative hook, and Benedict has been a fan favorite since the beginning. There is genuine reason to think Season 4 could represent an uptick.

What We Expect from Season 4

Based on the source material and the show's trajectory, here is what we anticipate for Season 4. Benedict has been the Bridgerton sibling most focused on art and self-discovery rather than societal duty, which gives the season a different emotional starting point than the previous three. The masquerade ball sequence is one of the most anticipated scenes in the entire book series, and the show's production design team has consistently delivered on spectacle.

Showrunner Jess Brownell has indicated that Season 4 will lean into the fairy-tale quality of Benedict's story while keeping the show's signature sensuality and social commentary. The class dynamics in the source novel — Benedict falls for a woman from a lower social station — provide natural material for the kind of societal critique that Bridgerton handles well.

Whether Season 4 can reverse the ratings decline remains to be seen, but the ingredients are promising. A beloved character, strong source material, an exciting new lead actress, and a clear narrative framework that distinguishes it from previous seasons.
