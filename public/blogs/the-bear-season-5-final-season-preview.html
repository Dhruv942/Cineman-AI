The Bear Season 5: Final Season Release Date, Plot & Cast Guide

Updated: May 29, 2026 10 min read

The Bear is ending. After four seasons of yelling-as-art, anxiety-as-cinematography, and the most accurate depiction of a professional kitchen ever put on television, FX and creator Christopher Storer have confirmed that Season 5 is the final season of The Bear. The good news: it's coming this summer. The complicated news: it picks up directly from one of the show's most polarizing cliffhangers.

Where Season 4 Left Off

Season 4 closed with Carmy doing the thing he'd been threatening for forty episodes: he stepped away. After the soft-launch of The Bear restaurant succeeded enough to keep the lights on, after Sydney finally made her call on the partnership offer, Carmy told the team he was leaving the food industry. The final shot was him handing over the keys, walking outside, and breathing.

Season 5 begins the morning after. Sydney, Richie, and Natalie are the new ownership trio. The Bear restaurant is open but unstable. And the chef whose name is on the building is gone.

What Season 5 Is About

Based on Storer's interviews and the show's narrative logic, three threads are clearly set up:

1. Can Sydney run a restaurant without Carmy?

Sydney has been the show's secret protagonist since Season 2. Season 5 is finally hers. The question isn't whether she's talented enough — the show has long settled that — but whether she wants to inherit the version of restaurant culture Carmy built. The early Season 4 hints that she'd rather rebuild from scratch are likely to pay off here.

2. What does Carmy do next?

One of two paths. Either Carmy stays out of the kitchen entirely and the show tracks his attempt to live a life that isn't 16-hour services, or he comes back in a different configuration — consultant, mentor, line cook somewhere else. Storer has hinted both options were on the writing-room board.

3. The Berzatto family resolution

Season 2's “Fishes” episode — widely considered the best single hour of TV in the 2020s — established the Berzatto family trauma as the show's emotional engine. Season 5 needs to give that some kind of resolution. Expect at least one major Donna (Jamie Lee Curtis) sequence and likely a Mikey-related reckoning.

Returning Cast

The core ensemble all return:

  • Jeremy Allen White as Carmen “Carmy” Berzatto
  • Ayo Edebiri as Sydney Adamu
  • Ebon Moss-Bachrach as Richie Jerimovich
  • Lionel Boyce as Marcus
  • Liza Colón-Zayas as Tina
  • Matty Matheson as Neil Fak
  • Abby Elliott as Natalie “Sugar” Berzatto
  • Jamie Lee Curtis as Donna Berzatto (recurring)
  • Bob Odenkirk as Uncle Lee (expected return)

The show's tradition of stunt-cast guest stars in single-episode appearances will almost certainly continue. Olivia Colman, John Mulaney, Will Poulter, and others have all rotated through previous seasons.

How The Bear Ratings Have Trended

This is where the show gets interesting. The Bear is one of the most-decorated TV series of the decade — 21 Primetime Emmys, including Outstanding Comedy Series wins — but its IMDb trajectory tells a more turbulent story.

Season 3 is the cautionary tale. Storer leaned hard into formal experimentation — long flashbacks, silent montages, episodes that barely visited the restaurant — and a meaningful chunk of the audience checked out. Season 4 walked it back. Season 5 needs to land both the family arc and the restaurant arc without losing its nerve.

Where to Watch

United States: Hulu, exclusively. All previous seasons are available there for catch-up.

International: Disney+ under the Star content banner (UK, EU, Canada, Australia, most international markets).

The Bear is one of those shows you genuinely should rewatch from the start. The first two seasons combined are under 18 hours, and the payoff for tracking Sydney's growth from new sous chef to potential restaurant owner is significant.
