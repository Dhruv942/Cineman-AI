Wicked: For Good (2025) IMDb Rating & Review

Published: May 15, 2026 10 min read

TL;DR

Wicked: For Good IMDb rating: 7.3 | Rotten Tomatoes: 80% critic, 95% audience | Metacritic: 71. Cynthia Erivo and Ariana Grande return for the second half of the Broadway adaptation. Wicked: For Good closes out Jon M. Chu's two-film adaptation of the Broadway musical. The first film, released November 2024, hit $750 million globally and pulled an IMDb 7.6 / 88% RT critic score. For Good arrived November 2025 with the harder job: adapting Act II, which most musical-theater fans agree is the weaker half of the original stage show.

The verdict from critics, audiences, and the IMDb numbers tells a consistent story: For Good is good but not great, weighed down by source-material problems the first film did not have but elevated by Erivo's awards-caliber performance and a final 30 minutes that genuinely lands.

The pattern is consistent across every source: a small but real step down from the first film, with the gap between critic and audience scores widening. That is exactly what you would expect from a film that adapts the messier half of a famously two-act musical.

What Wicked: For Good Is About

The film picks up where the first ended. Elphaba (Cynthia Erivo) has been branded the Wicked Witch of the West and is hiding in the wilderness, organizing resistance against the Wizard's regime. Glinda (Ariana Grande) has become the public face of Oz, engaged to Fiyero, and increasingly aware that her position is built on lies.

Dorothy arrives. The famous house drops. The events of The Wizard of Oz play out in the background of a story that reframes them entirely. By the end, the question is not "who is the wicked one" but "what does goodness even mean when the system itself is the villain."

It is heavy thematic material handled with mixed success. The film's emotional climax (Elphaba and Glinda's final duet) is genuinely moving. The connective tissue between musical numbers can feel labored.

What Works

Cynthia Erivo

Erivo's Best Actress nomination was earned. Her Elphaba in For Good is angrier, more isolated, and more commanding than in the first film. The vocal performance is among the strongest of her career — she takes "No Good Deed" and tears it apart in a way the Broadway version never quite did.

The Final 30 Minutes

The ending is genuinely great. The film earns its emotional payoff. Whatever you think of the middle section, the closing sequence delivers on the promise the first film set up. This is the kind of finale that makes you forgive structural issues earlier on.

Production Design and Costume

Both Oscar wins. The production design is exceptional — the Emerald City sequences in particular are stunning. Costume designer Paul Tazewell took home a deserved Oscar.

What Does Not Work

Pacing

The film is 137 minutes adapted from roughly 75 minutes of stage material. The padding shows. New original songs (added because the Broadway second act was considered slim) range from "fine" to "actively delaying the plot."

Source Material Problems

This is not the film's fault. Act II of Wicked has been the show's weak link since 2003. The plot becomes scattered, the romance with Fiyero gets resolved off-screen, and the parallel-Wizard-of-Oz framework requires a lot of expository connective tissue. The film inherits all of this.

Jonathan Bailey Underused

Fiyero (Bailey) is sidelined for much of the runtime, which the source material explicitly does. Bailey was the breakout charm of the first film, and the sequel never finds enough for him to do.

Should You Watch It?

Watch it if: you saw and liked the first film, you love musical theater (especially Wicked specifically), you want to see Erivo's Oscar-nominated performance, you appreciate ambitious if uneven adaptations.

Skip it if: you skipped or disliked the first film (this is a direct continuation), you have low tolerance for movie musicals, you found the first film's pacing slow (the second is slower).

For Good is not the equal of Wicked Part One. It is also not the disaster some critics anticipated. It is what it is — a faithful adaptation of a stage musical's weaker half, elevated by a great lead performance and a stronger-than-expected ending. The IMDb 7.3 is honest. If you loved the first film, you will be glad you saw the second.
